Cockroach infested London pub shut down and ordered to pay 1,300

August 21, 2024
blog

A pub infested with cockroaches has been told to shut down and pay £1,328.

Horrified health officials inspected the Plough and Harrow in Leytonstone, east London last week, and posted a video online of their disgusting finds.

The video shows kitchen draws full of dry food – and several cockroaches.

The inspection team also found more incidents of a lack of cleanliness and “poor” hygiene standards, My London reports.

Officers immediately closed the business due to the imminent risk posed to customers. On Thursday 6 April, Thames Magistrates Court granted a Hygiene Emergency Prohibition Order and awarded the Council its costs of £1328.

Costs of more than £1,300 were awarded to Waltham Forest Council during a hearing at Thames Magistrates Court on Thursday, April 6.

Inspectors described it as a "serious cockroach infestation" after they found the insects in several areas of the boozer.

As a result, Foggy Lake Ltd and Star of India restaurateur Shah Ahmed – who owns the pub - cannot operate a food service out of the premises until the “imminent risk” to the public is removed.

A Waltham Forest Council spokesman said: “This case underscores the importance of good food hygiene standards and the hard work of our team in protecting our residents from risks to health and safety.

“While the council continues to do everything within its means to remove these risks, we urge customers to check food hygiene ratings before eating out or ordering a takeaway.”

The pub's website still claims that the boozer is “open”, despite the closure from the council.

It claims that the food served at the site is Caribbean-inspired, and relaunched in December, 2021, as The Bronx Kitchen.

The venue also has its own music venue called Sinbin, which is in the same building, but the Twitter account for that venue was suspended for “violating” Twitter's rules.
