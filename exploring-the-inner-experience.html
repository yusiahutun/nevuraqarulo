Experiences generally refer to conscious events, activities, or perceptions and the knowledge that these events produce. Over the years, internal and external experiences have been researched and can be classified in a number of ways. Perceptual experiences, for example, mainly depict how we view the external world by the things we perceive and how we respond to these perceptions. On the other hand, an episodic experience entails remembering and reliving something that happened in the past. We imagine things in an imaginative experience, forming a mental image of something that isn’t felt in reality. 

The experience of thinking involves representing information mentally and processing it. For instance, experiencing pleasure signifies pleasant feelings like playing a game, going for a picnic, winning a sports bet, etc., while an experience of desire entails the feeling of wanting something. 1. What are internal experiences?

Inner experiences include our thoughts, ideas, feelings, reasoning, decision, sensations, desires, etc.; in short, anything that appears within the range of our consciousness. These experiences play a significant role in our day-to-day lives, our actions, and how decisions are taken. Essentially, the ability to think, feel, and sense things distinguish us as humans. 

These sensations entail what we think about, how we feel about a situation, what we concentrate on, ignore, etc. Thus, they influence our lives and define us as people while motivating our actions and words.

Because our inner experience is private to us and no one else, researching it is somewhat difficult. Furthermore, psychological scientists are divided on their position regarding inner experiences. Some say that researching this phenomenon is impossible and, at best, pointless, while others believe it is a rather simple phenomenon that can be determined by the accurate investigation. That is, you can find out what is on a person’s mind or what is going on with a person internally by just asking such individual specific questions about what they are thinking or feeling. 

2. How inner experiences define us

One central question repeatedly asked regarding conscious experiences is if they can be correctly described. Can we provide trustworthy reports of our sensory sensations and pains, inner voices, conversations, images in our minds, and the emotions we feel? This question is essential to our humanistic sense.

According to psychologists, five essential elements of inner experience tend to appear regularly across various disciplines. These elements include images, feelings, inner speech, sensory awareness, and unsymbolized thinking, which is a very controversial topic of discourse among psychologists. Unsymbolized thinking is a type of thinking that does not employ words, images, or any other internal representation or symbol. Some psychologists believe that it is impossible to think without using these inner symbols, making the idea of unsymbolized thinking impossible and nonexistent. 

Nevertheless, the level to which individuals use the various elements of inner experience varies substantially. For example, some people’s inner experience is dominated by images, while in others, it is by inner speech. Whichever the case, the various elements of inner experiences and how they relate to each individual may reflect the person’s personality. Persons who regularly use images, for instance, are thought to be likely imaginative and creative but not realistic. In contrast, people who frequently use inner speech are likely realistic but not as imaginative.

3. Every individual is a psychological being.

Understanding, reason, imagination, will, faculty of desire, and sensations of pleasure and sorrow, are all mental faculties assumed to be universally realized by every human. Therefore, a person must regard herself as a mind endowed with mental capacities such as the faculties for feeling, reasoning, and imagining in a world characterized by various distinct psychological characters. This will help apply the right decisions, emotions, and feelings to each situation that life brings. Using all these mental faculties in our judgment, relationships, and lifestyle generally shows that every individual is a psychological being.

In summary, Inner experience refers to a person’s conscious mental state, such as thoughts, feelings, sensory perceptions, imaginations, wants, actions, intentions, and memories of previous events. In addition, inner experience has broad effects on individual psychological features such as dispositions, personality traits, social interactions, moral, political, or religious ideals. 

According to this understanding, an individual is the distinct mental totality that is gradually developed via mental faculties. That is, the psychological phenomena that comprise inner experiences are what determine who we are.

Conclusion

Our individual inner experiences are part of what distinguishes us as humans. They play a central role in our intentions, action, and decisions and affect our lifestyle and relationship with others.
