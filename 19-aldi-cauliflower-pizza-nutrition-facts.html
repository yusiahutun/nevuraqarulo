FAQs

1. Are Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas gluten-free?

Yes, all Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas have a gluten-free crust made primarily from cauliflower, making them suitable for those with gluten sensitivities or preferences.

2. How many calories are in an Aldi Cauliflower Pizza?

The calorie content can vary depending on the flavor and toppings, but on average, a serving of Aldi Cauliflower Pizza has around 250-350 calories.

3. How many carbs are in Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas?

Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas are low in carbs compared to traditional pizzas. Each serving typically contains around 10-20 grams of carbs, making them a suitable option for those following a low-carb diet.

4. What are the main ingredients in Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas?

The main ingredients in Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas include cauliflower, cheese, eggs, and a selection of other fresh ingredients for the toppings, such as tomato sauce, meats, and vegetables.

5. Are Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas suitable for vegetarians?

Yes, there are vegetarian options available for Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas, such as Margherita or Veggie Delight, which are made with vegetarian-friendly toppings and do not contain any meat.

6. Can you freeze Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas?

Yes, you can freeze Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas. It’s recommended to wrap them tightly in aluminum foil or a freezer bag to maintain freshness. When ready to eat, simply thaw them in the fridge and follow the cooking instructions.

7. Where can I find Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas?

Aldi Cauliflower Pizzas are available at Aldi grocery stores. You can find them in the freezer section alongside other frozen pizzas and meals.
