Kenya has over 150 commercial radio stations according to data by the Communications Authority of Kenya.

Ghetto Radio

One of the radio stations that has gained popularity among the youth is Ghetto Radio.

According to its website, the station, which broadcasts its programmes using Sheng language, was launched in 2007.

Its content is inspired by urban social energies. It offers a voice for young Kenyans to champion their welfare.

Who owns Ghetto Radio?

The urban station is owned by Kenyan lawyer Maria Mbeneka, who is the wife of former Laikipia governor Ndiritu Muriithi.

Metropol TV reported Mbeneka runs the Ghetto Radio Foundation, which manages Ghetto Radio.

Julius Owino aka Maji Maji is the managing director of the Nairobi-based station.

Maria Mbeneka's profile

The lawyer has been in practice for over 15 years. She is a council member of the Commonwealth Lawyers Association (CLA) representing East Africa and Seychelles.

CLA is a membership organisation that brings together professional lawyers, academics and students practising within the Commonwealth.

It exists to maintain and promote the rule of law throughout the Commonwealth by ensuring that an independent and efficient legal profession serves its member countries.

The organisation undertakes its activities by encouraging exchanges between members of the profession through projects, conferences and workshops, as well as driving improvements in legal education.

According to CLA, Mbeneka holds a bachelor of social legislation degree (BSL) and a bachelor of laws degree from Dr Babasaheb Ambedkar Marathwada University, Maharashtra in India.

The astute lawyer holds a diploma in law from the Kenya School of Law and a certificate from the Mediators Training Institute MTI.

Between 2016 and 2018, she served as the vice president of the East Africa Law Society. She was also a member of the Industrial Property Tribunal from 2010 to 2013.

Kimani Kabucho Mbeneka & Company (KKM) Advocates

Mbeneka is a partner at Kimani Kabucho Mbeneka & Company (KKM) Advocates.

The law firm was established on April 12, 2001, with Eric Kimani as managing partner, as well as Stephen Karanja Kabucho and Mbeneka.

KKM Advocates has offices in Nairobi, Nanyuki, and Nyahururu and is working with various partners within the East African region.

Mbeneka's failed LSK bid

In 2020, Mbeneka vied to be the Law Society of Kenya (LSK) president but lost to seasoned lawyer Nelson Havi.

Havi emerged top in the hotly contested race that saw him win with a landslide after garnering 571 votes against Mbeneka's 161 votes.

Mbeneka had resigned from the Co-operative Tribunal under the Ministry of Agriculture to focus on LSK campaigns but unfortunately lost.

Kenyan lawyers who charge high legal fees

Law is one of the highest-paying jobs in Kenya with good returns.

According to Salary Explorer, a salary comparison and career resources website for both employees and employers, lawyers in Kenya take home an average of KSh 371,000 monthly.

They are ranked third behind doctors and judges. However, their pay depends on experience and the weight of the case.
