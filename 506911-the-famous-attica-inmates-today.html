The most famous Attica inmates and where they are today

June 15, 2024
blog

Attica is one of the renowned correctional centres in the United States. It was constructed in the 1930s in response to earlier riots within the New York state prisons. The facility has housed some notable criminals. So, who are the most famous Attica inmates?

Famous Attica inmates are notable because of the nature of crimes that led them to imprisonment. They earned their sentence primarily because of armed robbery or murder. Some are still serving their sentence to date.

Most famous Attica inmates

Why is Attica famous? Attica Correctional Facility is a maximum security prison in Attica, New York. The New York State Department of Corrections and Community Supervision operates it.

Who is the most famous prisoner in Attica? It has housed some of the most famous criminals in history. Some of these notable inmates include:

1. David Berkowitz

David Richard Berkowitz, alia Son of Sam and .44 Caliber Killer, is an American serial killer. He pleaded guilty to eight shootings that happened in New York City in 1976. He was sentenced to Attica, and he was there by 1979.

2. Kendall Francois

Kendall Francois was a serial killer from Poughkeepsie, New York. He was convicted of killing eight women between 1996 to 1998. He was housed in the Attica Correctional Facility until being transferred to the Wende Correctional Facility.

Kendall died in 2014, shortly after being transferred to the Wende Prison. It was revealed in his trial in 2000 that he tested positive for HIV in 1995. However, this was not said to be related to his death.

4. Willie Sutton

William Francis Sutton Jr. was an American bank robber. His forty-year robbery career saw him steal an estimated $2 million. He spent over half of his adult life in prison and escaped three times.

Willie's talent at executing robberies in disguises earned him two nicknames, Willie the Actor and Slick Willie. He died on November 2nd, 1980, at the age of 79.

5. Edward Cummiskey

Edward Cummiskey is one of the famous Attica Correctional Facility inmates. He was a New York mobster who served as a mentor to Jimmy Coonan, leader of the Westies. He is remembered for his infamous act of showing Coonan how to dismember and dispose of murder victims.

They scattered their victims' remains into New York's waterfronts, mainly in the Hudson River. Cummiskey died on August 20th, 1976, in a bar. Hitman Joseph Sullivan murdered him.

6. Mark David Chapman

Mark David Chapman is an American criminal. He came into the limelight because of murdering former Beatles member John Lennon in New York City on December 8th, 1980. He shot Lennon from a few yards away with a Charter Arms Undercover .38 Special revolver.

7. Colin Ferguson

Colin Ferguson was born in Kingston, Jamaica, on January 14th, 1958, to Von Herman and May Ferguson. He is one of the infamous criminals who have been housed at the Attica correctional facility.

He is known for his infamous act on December 7th, 1993. He pulled out a 9mm pistol and started firing at other passengers when a Long Island Rail Road train pulled into the Merillon Avenue station in Garden City, New York.

Ferguson murdered six people and wounded 19 others before being stopped by other passengers. He was sentenced to 315 years and eight months to life. He is serving the sentence at the Upstate Correctional Facility in Franklin County, New York.

8. H. Rap Brown

Jamil Abdullah Al-Amin, alias H. Rap Brown, is a civil rights activist, black separatist, and convicted murderer. Previously, he had been arrested in 1971, put on trial for armed robbery, and sent to Attica.

He is serving a life sentence for murder following the shooting of two Fulton County Sheriff's deputies in 2000. He is housed at The United States Penitentiary, Tucson.

9. Sam Melville

Samuel Joseph Melville was the principal conspirator and bomb setter in the 1969 bombings of eight government buildings in New York City. He was a key figure in the 1971 Attica Prison riots. He was shot by the police and killed when the uprising was put down by force.

10. Dean Faiello

Dean Faiello is an American criminal. He was formerly imprisoned at Attica Correctional Facility for the April 2003 death of Maria Cruz. He had presented himself to her as a dermatologist.

11. Joseph 'Mad Dog' Sullivan

Who escaped from Attica? Joseph 'Mad Dog' Sullivan was the prisoner who attempted to flee from Attica. He was born on March 31st, 1939, in Queens, New York, and became a Mafia hitman, incarcerated at Attica prison.

In Attica Correctional Facility history, he was the only person to escape in 1971. He was recaptured and, after being released on parole, was imprisoned again on three murder convictions. He killed Teamsters Vice President John Fiorino.

12. Jimmy Caci

Vincent Dominic "Jimmy" Caci was a Los Angeles crime family member and a Caporegime in the family. He was born on August 1st, 1925, in Westfield, New York, to Alfonzo and Josephine Caci.

Jimmy grew up in Western New York. In the 1970s, he spent eight years at Attica prison for armed robbery. In the late 1970s, he moved to Southern California. He died on August 16th, 2011, at the Eisenhower Medical Center in Rancho Mirage, California.

13. Jerry Rosenberg

Jerome Rosenberg was a New York State convict, mobster, and jailhouse lawyer. He was incarcerated for 46 years, longer than any other prisoner in New York State history. He was sentenced to death for his involvement in the 1962 killing of two police officers.

Jerry Rosenberg was spared the death penalty for his crime. However, he eventually died on June 1st, 2009, aged 72.

14. Valentino Dixon

Valentino Dixon grew up in East Buffalo, New York, the inner city. He was born on October 20th, 1969, and was his mother's only son. In 1991, a fight broke out in front of a Buffalo hot dog joint. One person died, and the police later arrested Valentino Dixon for the shooting.

Valentino was ultimately sentenced to 38.5 years in prison. He served most of this sentence at Attica. After 27 years in prison, Dixon was found innocent and released.
