Soho Farmhouse, Oxfordshire

February 15, 2024
blog

Pictures courtesy of Soho Farmhouse

If you're an Instagram user, then chances are you will have seen an influx of pictures from Soho Farmhouse, the new Oxfordshire retreat from the Soho House group, on your feed over the past few months. In actual fact, the private members' group doesn't actively encourage social media – preferring instead to err on the side of discretion – but, from the moment you arrive at the welcome gate (to get ferried to your cabin via either converted vintage milk float or horse and cart), it's pretty hard to resist sharing... 

Accomodation

Set within 100 acres of Oxfordshire countryside, Soho Farmhouse features 40 cabins of varying sizes – plus a four-bedroom cottage and seven-bedroom farmhouse for larger parties. While there's plenty to do on site, the cabins seem designed to make it as hard as possible to leave them. From the kitchen stocked with home-made cookies and Soho House cheese to the living area with wood burner (and blankets – there are blankets everywhere), you could quite easily cosy up in your romantic bolthole for an entire weekend and not see another living soul. 

Activities

Of course, you wouldn't want to. Each cabin comes with a customised Foffa bike for each occupant (complete with name tag) which allows you to explore the site fully. And there's a lot on offer. There's a state-of-the-art gym and both indoor and outdoor swimming pools. There's an on-site stableyard offering horse-riding and pony trekking. There's a comprehensive Cowshed spa offering a huge variety of treatments; and a Josh Wood hair salon for grooming, colouring and styling; a deli offering home-reared charcuterie; a wine cellar; a cookery school; a five-a-side football pitch; tennis courts; and an ice rink in the winter. There's even a cinema. Oh, and a boating lake.

Eating and drinking

Of course, the Soho House group is renowned for its dining, thanks to its hugely successful outposts in London (and internationally – from New York to Istanbul). The options at Soho Farmhouse are worth the visit alone. The main farmhouse offers all-day dining for families, with adult-only mezzanine levels – think classic British comfort food enjoyed next to an open fire. Fancy Farm is a fine-dining option for a more elegant evening, while the Mill Room offers a country-pub vibe with a variety of beers and ales on tap (and stays open until the last man's standing – the record being 7.30am). A rotating restaurant in the middle of the main courtyard also has a weekly changing chef residency; offering Indian cuisine one week and Italian the next. 

Private hire

The farmhouse has several areas available for private hire; the Barwell Barn can host a dinner for up to 65 people, the Farmyard Deli is best for more intimate dinners of around 20 guests, while the Hay Barn and Marquee Green can cater for larger parties of up to 250. The entire site can also play host to beautiful countryside weddings.

Wow factor

The attention to detail is what makes a stay at Soho Farmhouse so special. Guests are asked for their height and shoe size when booking, to ensure the correct bike and pair of wellies are waiting for them. Regulars will also be greeted with their favourite cocktail upon arrival at the gatehouse.

For those who like a weekend retreat with the same comforts of the city, it's ideal (and incredibly Instagrammable).
