Big Brother Australia: Krystal Forscutt reveals surprise bonus of having her breast implants removed

She had her breast implants removed last month after suffering a rupture while pregnant with her third child.

And Krystal Hipwell (née Forscutt) revealed on Thursday she was enjoying the benefits of having a smaller chest.

The Big Brother star, 35, said on Instagram she no longer has to worry about 'a boob popping out' while chasing her children around.

Krystal, a former glamour model, explained she'd spent the day playing beach soccer and hadn't slipped out of her bikini top once.

'How good is being able to play beach soccer without worrying about a boob popping out!' she captioned a photo of herself in a black two-piece.

She added the hashtag #SmallBoobPerks.

Krystal continued: '[It] also turns out I'm pretty much the same crop size as my three-year-old daughter now, but I'm not mad about it.'

The former reality star had her implants removed last month and told fans the explant surgery marked the start of a 'new chapter' in her life.

She said she first got her implants 'as a young girl searching for approval' and grew to resent them.

Krystal clarified she didn't have a problem with plastic surgery, but hinted she underwent a breast enlargement at the age of 19 to please other people, not herself. 

While she'd been considering getting them removed for a while, her decision to have surgery was triggered by a rupture in one implant while pregnant with her son.

She has since recommended an ultrasound for women who are experiencing pain or who have had implants for 'longer than 10 years'.

Krystal rose to fame as a contestant on the 2006 season of Big Brother Australia alongside her mother, Karen Forscutt.

She went on to pose for several lads' magazines, including Zoo Weekly and Ralph, but retired from glamour modelling at the age of 23.

Years later she abandoned her racy image and became a devoted Christian after meeting her husband, property developer Neil Hipwell.

Neil took her to his local church on their second date, an experience she later described as 'life changing'.

By the time Neil encouraged her to convert to Christianity, Krystal had grown 'tired' of her glamour-model lifestyle.

'I got to a point where I was completely over it,' she told New Idea magazine.

'I took so many opportunities purely because they were there. They fell at my feet and I took them. I never really thought some of them through properly.'

The Hipwells are parents to three children, Nakoda, two, Sunny, six, and Huska, one.
