They say imitation is the best form of flattery, and so fans of ITV's detective show Lewis will appreciate the final nod to its predecessor Inspector Morse.

The final scene of the series will mimic the last moment of Morse, which saw the late John Thaw throwing in his police badge, over a pint with his younger sergeant, Robert Lewis.

But instead of Kevin Whately's Detective Inspector announcing his retirement, it's his sidekick DS James Hathaway - played by Laurence Fox - who is ending the show by leaving the force.

Tribute: The final episode of ITV detective series Lewis replicates the last scene of its predecessor Morse

Sat across from each other in one of the Oxford-setting beer gardens, the two nurse a pint across a wooden table.

Overlooking the Thames the moment replicates the end of Morse 12 years earlier in 2000 and some say this is the perfect tribute to Thaw who died in 2002.

'The ending is a beautiful and brilliant nod to the past and to where Lewis came from,' a source told The Daily Mirror.

Farewell: John Thaw and Kevin Whately shared a pint and an orange juice at the moment Morse throws in his police badge for good

Joint decision: Both Laurence and Kevin chose not to continue in the detective drama, but their characters will not be killed off

Both Fox and Whately had made the decision together to call time on the series, with Kevin saying they needed 'a break' at the start of the year.

'We said to ITV last November that we’d like to stop,' the actor explained. 'They said, "We’re not killing you" so this series comes to a kind of stop.'

Therefore it may not be the end for the Oxford based duo, with reports that some more episodes could come into production next year.

Obviously that may depend on just how successful Laurence is in America, as he and his wife Billie Piper are moving to Hollywood with their two kids to break into the movie industry.

Not that the Yorkshire-born actor is a stranger to the film industry, with his first starring role opposite Kiera Knightley and Thora Birch in 2001 teen thriller The Hole.

Since then he's appeared in a number of British period features including Gosford Park, Becoming Jane and Elizabeth II: The Golden Age.

The last episode of Lewis airs on ITV1 on February 11 at 9pm.
