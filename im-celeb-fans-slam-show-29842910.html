I'm A Celeb fans slam show format after realising major issue with ITV spin-off

February 16, 2024

I'm A Celebrity fans have hit out at the show's South African format after spotting a fresh issue with the series.

Viewers have been left frustrated with the ITV show and said they are "taking the mick with the adverts."

It comes following the launch of the spin-off series, which welcomed campmates including Jordan Banjo, Amir Khan, Helen Flanagan, Carol Vorderman, Fatima Whitbread and more.

While a third complained: "So grateful I recorded #ImACeleb so I don't have to suffer all the adverts."

But that wasn't the only issue, as others stressed they couldn't get used to the change in season.

"This feels so wrong without Christmas adverts #ImACeleb," someone wrote.

Another viewer added: "Watching #ImACeleb and waiting on Christmas adverts!"

Some fans debated their thoughts about the programme not being live.

One person said: "The Ant and Dec part of I’m a celeb not being live is weirding me out and I don’t know why."

Another commented: "I think they’ll find viewers start to turn off because there’s no power of the vote everything’s already decided. Won’t be long before the winner is leaked."

The show also features; Paul Burrell, Phil Tufnell, Janice Dickinson, Shaun Ryder, Gillian McKeith, Georgia Toffolo, Andy Whyment and Myleene Klass.

Rumours have surfaced that ex-campmate Olivia Attwood could return to the jungle.

It comes after a source told the Daily Mail: “Olivia’s time in the jungle last year was to give her a platform so that ITV could give her a big show.

“They had a huge future mapped out for her. She’s a big draw for the much-sought-after younger viewers. Then it all came to an end after her blood tests came back.

“Now they are getting her back for this year and will start the whole plan again. ITV bosses know she will be so popular on I’m A Celeb... and that viewers will just love her.”
