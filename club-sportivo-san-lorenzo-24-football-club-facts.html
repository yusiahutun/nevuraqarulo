The Birth of Club Sportivo San Lorenzo

Club Sportivo San Lorenzo, commonly referred to as San Lorenzo, is an iconic football club based in Buenos Aires, Argentina. Founded on April 1, 1908, this renowned club has a rich history and a passionate fan base.

The Club’s Historic Stadium

Club Sportivo San Lorenzo plays its home games at the Estadio Pedro Bidegain, also known as the “Nuevo Gasómetro.” The stadium, located in the Boedo neighborhood of Buenos Aires, has a capacity of over 47,000 spectators. It has witnessed countless memorable moments throughout the years, including championship victories and intense derby matches.

A Legacy of Success

San Lorenzo has achieved significant success both domestically and internationally. The club has won numerous titles, including 15 Primera División championships, the most recent being in the 2013 Torneo Inicial. San Lorenzo’s most significant international achievement came in 2014 when they won the prestigious Copa Libertadores, South America’s most coveted club competition.
