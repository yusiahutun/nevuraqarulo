Candy Montgomery trial and charges

After the arrest, Candy was represented by defence attorney Robert Udashen and civil law attorney. The trial was held in McKinney, Texas, for eight days and was presided over by District Judge Tom Ryan.

She pleaded that it was self-defence after Gore tried attacking her during a confrontation about her affair with her husband, Allan. Candy claimed that she was compelled to use the axe after Gore tried to strike her with the same axe moments before she got a hold of it.

Before her trial, she underwent a polygraph test which proved that she was truthful with her defence statement. However, the District attorney, Tom O'Connell, argued that the attack wasn't necessary as she could have fled from the scene instead.

In addition, the attorney argued that attacking Betty 41 times was inappropriate. On 29th October 1980, a jury of nine women and three men declared her not guilty. However, the verdict was received with much criticism from the community, who felt she was guilty of murdering Betty.
