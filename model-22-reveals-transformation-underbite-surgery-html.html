Model, 22, reveals transformation after underbite surgery

July 01, 2024
blog

A model who struggled to chew and breathe due to her prominent chin caused by an extreme underbite is now preparing for a top beauty pageant after undergoing gruelling jaw surgery.

Sofia Mayers, whose family joked she looked like Bruce Forsyth, underwent the arduous operation that saw her gum line sliced, her top and bottom jaws broken, realigned and secured in place with metal plates and screws.

Shocking photos show how the 22-year-old's face quadrupled in size and she was left looking like she'd been brawling 'with Mike Tyson and The Rock' after the surgery.

Before the operation, Sofia, from Ashford, Kent, struggled to breathe and chew due to her misaligned jaws and teeth - which made going on dates 'impossible' for fear of food falling out of her mouth.

Now five months on, after winning a beauty queen title for the hometown, Sofia is still healing and is setting her sights on a national pageant later this year.

Sofia said: 'I've always had an underbite and when I was younger it was very endearing. My family would say "ooh Brucey, it's [the underbite] really cute" as in Bruce Forsyth, it was a really sweet family joke.

'It was always really funny but as I got older I was like "I don't really want to have a big chin and be compared to a man in his 80s". He was a national treasure - but still.

'I wasn't necessarily really self-conscious of it but it was something my orthodontist and dentist were aware of. Unbeknown to me it was affecting my breathing, my chewing and all my eating and digestion.'

But years of wearing braces pushed her bottom teeth forwards and upper teeth backwards so much that it created a massive three-finger wide gap.

This meant she was unable to chew properly, keep food in her mouth or speak without a lisp.

Sofia said: 'The way that they surgically put it was that I had quite "strong" features.

'I had a very definite nose and a very sharp and angular face - perfect for modelling, they loved it - but it was very angular. I didn't choose to have the surgery for cosmetic reasons, it was more the medical benefits from it.

'My braces moved my bottom jaw forward even more - so pushed my teeth forward - and then moved my upper teeth backwards and created a really big gap.

'Before I had the surgery I could fit three of my fingers in my mouth with my mouth open normally, it made eating impossible because my teeth were in the wrong position.

WHAT IS AN UNDERBITE?

An underbite occurs when a person's lower teeth and jaw protrude over their upper teeth.

This is usually inherited from a parent who also has the condition.

Other causes include thumb sucking, babies using dummies and prolonged bottle feeding. 

Aside from a sufferer's appearance, other symptoms can include headaches, poor self-esteem and teeth grinding or breaking. 

Braces are the most common treatment but can make wearers, particularly children, feel self conscious.

Specially-made headgears can be effective but are highly noticeable and often uncomfortable.

In extreme cases, surgery to shave off the jaw bone is required as a last resort.

It is unclear how many people suffer from an underbite in the UK or US.

Due to being on a predominantly liquid diet, naturally petite Sofia's weight plummeted from 8st to 6.5 stone in a matter of weeks.

Sofia said: 'I'm naturally a slim person and I was on a mostly liquid diet for two months. I went in for surgery around a healthy 8st - my weight dropped down to 6.5 stone in a couple of weeks - I looked like a skeleton.

'I'm vegan, which makes it really hard, and I'm allergic to gluten so trying to find foods that were easily liquidised, had lots of calories in them that were vegan and gluten-free [was difficult].

'I'm put off mashed potatoes for life because I had liquid mashed potato, I highly recommend not eating it because it's disgusting.'

Looking to the future, Sofia is focusing on her pageant ambitions and has her eye on a top UK gong, that would mean she could compete on the international stage.

Sofia said: 'If I was fortunate enough to win the national title, they fly you out to Indonesia to compete for one of the biggest titles in the world.

'It's one of the top five grand slams, it's a very prestigious pageant, so I'm going to give it a go and see how I get on.'

Now Sofia wants to share her stunning transformation to inspire others suffering with extreme under or overbites to take the plunge and seek help - but warns it isn't a 'quick fix'.

Sofia said: 'It's a major op to consider, for me it was medical. I knew that I needed the surgery, I might not have wanted it at the time but it's over and done with now.

'I haven't seen the full results of it yet. Reflecting back on my time having my little swollen turnip face I was like "it's not worth it".

'I can breathe a lot better now, I'm still learning to chew but it's a long process - they said it could take a year to recover.

'I'm glad it's over and done with. Cosmetically speaking I do like my side profile now, I think it suits my face a lot more.

'I've got much softer features, my side profile - my nose, chin and cheeks - are different. It doesn't look like the same person. Fingers crossed I'm getting my braces off in April.

'I'll always look back and still love my little wonky face, it's a very big change and I'm still getting used to it. It's not just an overnight quick fix. Anyone considering having it done needs to consider these different aspects of it.'
