Woman comes forward as the winner of $522MILLION lottery prize three months after the draw

September 27, 2024
blog

A Californian woman has come forward as the winner of an astonishing $522million lottery prize - three months after the draw. 

Laarni Bibal purchased a $2 Mega Millions ticket from the Sorrento Deli-Mart in San Diego in June, but hid her ticket in a 'secret hiding place' until she was ready to make her claim.

She had selected random quick pick numbers for the ticket: 27, 68, 17, 19 and 40 with 2 as her Mega number, the San Diego Union-Tribute reported.

The day after the drawing on June 7, Bibal discovered a winning ticket had been purchased from the very same store. But it wasn't until September 17 that lottery officials announced a winner had come forward.

Laarni Bibal, from California, has come forward as the winner of a $522million lottery prize three months after the June 7 draw (stock image)

'My heart started beating really fast!', Bibal told California State Lottery, recalling the moment she saw her numbers had been drawn.

But the lottery winner, whose age and location is unknown, decided to delay claiming her cash, keeping her ticket in a 'secret' hiding spot.

'I can't tell you,' Bibal said. 'The secret hiding place is still a secret!' 

Officials also revealed Bibal had consulted the Lottery Winners Handbook for information on how to handle her newfound riches. 

Her ticket was worth $522million, officials said, and Bibal opted for the cash option - which granted her $340million before federal taxes.

The winning $2 Mega Millions ticket was purchased from the Sorrento Deli-Mart in San Diego

She plans to use the money to purchase a new home and pay off her bills.

The market from which the ticket was purchased, on Roselle Street, also received a payout of $1million as their share of the prize.

Speaking in June, co-owner Mazen Mikhael described how his daughter woke him and his wife after the draw to tell them a winning ticket had come from their store. 

'It's just awesome. It's a dream come true. We've been selling tickets a long time, but nothing like this,' he said.
