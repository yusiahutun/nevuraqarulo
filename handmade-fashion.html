The best independent brands creating handmade fashion

February 07, 2024
blog

Agata Pospieszynska for Harper's Bazaar

Emma Brewin

MORE

Loved by the likes of Rosie Huntington-Whiteley, Rihanna, Kendall Jenner, Bella Hadid, and Emily Ratajkowski, Emma Brewin's instantly recognisable fuzzy delights are top of our favourite handmade brand list. Her fluffy hats – which come in all different shapes and colours – are handmade to order in England, and are shipped worldwide.

Hai

MORE

Hai began in 2018 when founder Tessa Vermeulen would spend her days sewing and designing the silk bags that would fast become a staple of the brand. Five years on, the label shows at London Fashion Week and has a loyal fanbase here in the UK, in Seoul and with recent store pop-ups in New York.

What makes the label so popular? Its penchant for delicate silk and feminine silhouettes, as well as whimsical details from lace, pearls and velvets. Hai is a special label to know, creating clothes to make you feel something.

Rooper

MORE

Rooper reminds us to have fun with fashion. The female-founded label – which specialises in bags and accessories – aims to inject vibrancy into the every day. Rooper's most well-know product, the Furoshiki bag, is inspired by traditional Japanese wrapping cloths.

Founder Natasha Fernandes-Anjo heads up a small team in Rooper's Manchester studio, creating entirely vegan products, while limiting any wastage and lowering its production footprint as its fabrics are sourced in limited quantities. This is part of Rooper's charm, once they're gone they're gone.

Realms

MORE

Laura Osborn launched her label in 2021, after years of working for designers such as Roksanda Ilincic, Roland Mouret and The Vampire’s Wife. Her pieces offer a unique ethereal spin on suiting and dresses, taking inspiration from medieval dressing and the mid-century. It's no wonder that the fashion industry and celebrity world is taking note of Realms, with Daisy-Edgar Jones wearing one of its trademark suits in a delicate powdered blue.

Good Squish

MORE

Designer Billie-Jo Cronin started her brand Good Squish in 2021 in her living room, using her grandma's table linen. The brand has grown dramatically since then, and now has its own Hackney-based studio; it specialises in timeless pieces made out of deadstock fabrics. We're putting every one of their scrunchies on our wishlist.

Olivia Rose The Label

MORE

Olivia Rose the Label began back in 2017 - with every single piece being designed and handmade to order by founder and designer Olivia Rose Havelock from her studio in Edinburgh. She offers made-to-measure pieces, which helps to reduce the waste of materials and means she can cater to any size. Havelock sources and purchases all fabrics locally in the UK for her slow-fashion label, which is also stocked at Selfridges.

Havelock's grandmother was her biggest inspiration for starting her own label; she was the first person to introduce her to a sewing machine when she was just six years old, and she's been making clothes ever since, with some of her first designs for her brand being made using the sewing machine her grandmother left for her.

Isla de Gar

MORE

Isla de Gar is a brand that is all about having some fun with fashion. Founded in the summer of 2020, designer Emma Garner creates sculptural, colourful designs which are handmade to order in its London studio by Garner and her assistant Bella.

The brand first became known for its 'Hive' design, and soon added other shapes to the collection that are all inspired by the natural world, including the 'Cocoon', 'Nest' and the 'Squid' bags.

Sandralexandra

MORE

Sandralexandra’s delicate jewellery is handmade by local artisans in her motherland Spain, using raw materials such as glass, metals and pearls. Her funky pieces – from brightly coloured fruits to heart motifs that look good enough to eat – are loved and adored by many, holding a special place in your jewellery box.
