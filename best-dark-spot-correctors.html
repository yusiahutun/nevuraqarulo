What causes dark spots?

There are different causes, from old pimples to sun exposure to scarring. “Any inflammation in the skin can also lead to a light or dark mark, which can subsequently be worsened following sun exposure," says Finney. "The most common cause of discoloration depends on age, with acne of past reigning supreme in younger patients, UV damage in older patients, and many other factors in between.”

What are the best treatments for dark spots?

Some of the best active ingredients to fade dark spots include niacinamide, kojic acid, and vitamin C. Hydroquinone is also a helpful ingredient, but is now only available by prescription as it can cause side effects.

In addition, it can't be overstated how much sun exposure worsens discoloration—and leads to new dark spots. “One of the least acknowledged truths about treating hyperpigmentation is that sunscreen is the very first step in improving them,” says Robinson. “Sunscreen is extremely important in protecting the skin from harmful UVA/UVB rays and also visible light, which drives hyperpigmentation in melanin-rich skin more than other skin tones. In these cases, sunscreen is more than just protective, it is essential in the treatment of hyperpigmentation.”
