Deputy President William Ruto has confirmed the passing on of his Chief of Staff Ken Osinde.

Osinde died at The Nairobi Hospital where he had been admitted for a week prior to his death on Friday, November 10.

The details of his illness remain scanty although sources close to the Office of the Deputy President intimated he had been battling liver complications for the last couple of months.

In his condolence message to the bereaved family, Ruto eulogised the deceased former ambassador as a leader with robust management skills and a great problem solver.

"My office has lost one of its key officers. Ambassador Ken Nyauncho Osinde was a good leader with robust management and problem-solving skills. As our Chief of Staff, he was a hardworking, diligent, broad thinker and a master of human resources," Ruto said.

Barely three weeks ago, Osinde represented Ruto at the closing ceremony of the 7th Devolution Conference in Makueni county.

Osinde, however, was turned away by governors who faulted the DP for being disrespectful.

Ruto had been slated to preside over the closing ceremony of the event held in Makueni county on Friday, November 26.

However, the deputy president snubbed the event, instead sending his Chief of Staff, Osinde, to represent him.

Osinde was stopped from presenting Rut's speech, the governors saying, "the event is an international one and not everyone can preside over the closing ceremony".

"We expected the DP to attend and preside over the closing ceremony. As governors, we consider this to be an international event and not every person can execute such an important function," said Governor Wycliffe Oparanya of Kakamega.

Ruto's office had communicated regarding his failure to attend the event on its final day, leaving the governors in limbo over their next course of caution.

After many years in academia as a lecturer, Osinde took up the post of manager at the National Bank of Kenya, where he rose to the position of Head of the Strategic Planning Group.

In 2010, retired president Mwai Kibaki appointed him as Kenya's ambassador to Germany.
