Top 15 black female gospel singers of all time

May 02, 2024
blog

Gospel music industry is growing exponentially with new artists making entry every year. However, while the number of male artists is growing, black female gospel singers are less predominant in the top-performing charts. The golden opportunity has been afforded to female artists through the growth of digital platforms and media.

Gospel, just like other genres of music, is adapting and growing depending on demand and media. One of the biggest threats lies in the disparity of gender distribution which is still gaping. How many women gospel singers do you know of?

List of black female gospel singers

The list of top female gospel artists comprises of artists in the older days and those who have risen to the helm of their music careers. Who is the best female gospel singer in the world? Here is a list of the top 15 female gospel singers:

1. Tasha Cobbs

Tasha is an award-winning gospel musician and songwriter. She began her career in 2010 with her self-released album known as Smile. She has a crystal-clear mezzo-soprano voice, no wonder many people love her songs. Some of her other hit songs are Fill Me Up, You Still Love Me, Break Every Chain and Your Spirit, among others.

2. Tamela Mann

Tamela Mann is known to many for her refreshing and life-giving music. She started her career back in 1999 as a co-singer with Kirk Franklin. She is not only a talented musician but also an award-winning artist having bagged the Dove Award during her rewarding career.

3. Jamie Grace

Jamie is a gospel artist who was born from a family of two pastors. She started singing at a young age of 11 years and has never yielded to despair from changing demands. She graduated from college in May 2012, further spicing up her already flourishing gospel music career.

4. Mandisa

Mandisa is a down-to-earth gospel artist whose career has grown from being a backup singer to a well-to-do musician. Sparrow Records signed her in 2007 after making to the top nine of the American Idol Contest.

5. Vanessa Bell Armstrong

Vanessa is a celebrated gospel artist who made her debut as a solo singer in 1983 with the hit Peace Be Still which is her signature song. Vanessa's second album known as Chosen topped the US Billboard Top Gospel Albums chart. Her firm, powerful and smooth voice is easily recognizable through her songs such as Teach Me Oh Lord, Pressing on and Good News, among others.

6. Cece Winans

Cece Winans is one of the best female worship singers you will ever think about. Other than being one of the best female vocalists ever, she has led revivals around the world, transforming the hearts of believers and changing lives.

7. Yolanda Adams

Yolanda Adams is not your everyday female artist. She is one of the best female vocalists in the gospel industry and a radio host. She has defied odds to become one of the best black female gospel artists of all time through amazing lyrics and consistency.

8. Sinach

Sinach is by far the current best black female gospel singer in the world. Her songs, including Waymaker, Shout to the Lord, Waymaker among others have earned her accolades across the globe and changed the lives of men.

9. Vickie Winans

Vickie Winans is a black gospel artist whose career has been defined by growth. Her albums Be Encouraged, Total Victory, and The Vickie Winas: Laugh have significantly been pivotal in her career.

10. Shirley Ceasar

Shirley is a black female gospel artist who has transformed the gospel industry through her life-changing lyrics and transformative messages. Other than being one of the greatest gospel artists of all time, she has also been a member of the second-best gospel singers’ groups in the world.

11. Karen Clark Sheard

Karen is one of the young female gospel singers who have produced life-changing gospel music. Some of her works include Destined to Win, Finally Karen, Church Girl, Only call on Jesus, among others. She is a vessel of good news, traversing through the world with good news.

12. Mahalia Jackson

Mahalia is from the older days, but her music still lives on to date. She was not only an artist but also one of the most influential people of all time.

13. Twinkle Clark

Twinkle Clark is a famous gospel singer, songwriter and evangelist. She is also famous for her tenure with The Clark Sisters and for being a member of the Hammond Organ Hall of Fame. The group is known for songs such as Expect Your Miracle, Jesus is a Love Song, and Is My Living in Vain.

14. Kierra Kiki Sheard

Kierra Sheard is an award-winning gospel artist. Other than singing, she is also a songwriter, fashion designer, creative director, entrepreneur, and actress.

15. Dorina Clarke Cole

Dorina is a gospel artist and talented songwriter. Some of her works include Living It, Bless this House, and He Brought Me among others.

Gospel music has not only transformed the lives of people but also impacted generations. It is great to see black female gospel singers take the lead in sharing the good news through their talents.
