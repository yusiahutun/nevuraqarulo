Biography TimeLine

EST Gee, born in Louisville, Kentucky, is an American rapper known for his unique blend of trap, gangsta rap, and drill music. He gained popularity in the early 2010s by collaborating with fellow Kentucky rapper Jack Harlow. Gee’s music often draws on his experiences growing up in poverty, dealing drugs, and navigating the streets of Louisville. With his raw lyrics, accurate delivery, and charismatic presence, EST Gee has become a rising star in the rap world.

  • 1994: EST Gee was born in Louisville, Kentucky, on August 11.
  • 2017: Gee began his music career in earnest, releasing his first single, “Ball Forever,” in September.
  • 2018: Gee released his debut mixtape, “I Am EST,” in March. The mixtape featured collaborations with Jack Harlow, 2KBaby, and other local artists.
  • 2019: Gee continued to gain traction in the rap world, releasing his second mixtape, “Bigger Than Life or Death,” in November. The mixtape included features from Yo Gotti, Lil Durk, and other notable artists.
  • 2020: Gee’s popularity grew, and he signed a record deal with Yo Gotti’s CMG label in August. He released his third mixtape, “Ion Feel Nun,” later that year, which included collaborations with Young Thug, 42 Dugg, and others.
  • 2021: EST Gee’s star continued to rise in 2021, with the release of his fourth mixtape, “Loyal Bros,” in July. The mixtape debuted at number 49 on the Billboard 200 chart, marking Gee’s first entry. He also made high-profile collaborations with rappers such as Lil Baby, DaBaby, and Future. Gee’s momentum led him to sign another major record deal with Interscope Records in October, further solidifying his position in the rap industry.
