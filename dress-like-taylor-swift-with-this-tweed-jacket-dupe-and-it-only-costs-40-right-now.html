Dress like Taylor Swift with tweed jacket dupe that only costs $40 right now

June 18, 2024
blog

Taylor Swift is in her element, bringing tweed coats back into fashion, and Swifties can achieve the same look on a budget.

After wrapping up her South American leg of Eras tour, Taylor Swift was spotted in New York for a cozy dinner date at Paros restaurant. Amid rumors that the singer is moving in with Travis Kelce, Tay Tay showed up in NYC looking chic in a matching neutral tweed jacket and skirt.

Her new look hints that Reputation Taylor’s Version is coming soon, as she plays with more neutrals and black and white.

Taylor Swift is bringing back tweed jacket fashion for winter

Swifties are ready to file a petition to see Taylor Swift in more long coats and tweed jackets, as the music legend looks phenomenal in her new dinner-date look. The Ready For It crooner paired her iconic red lip with a £2,2000 Stella McCartney tweed coat and matching skirt, along with a black top and black sheer stockings.

Tay Tay is definitely in her playful fashion era, as she donned a pair of Jimmy Choo and Jean Paul Gaultier’s collaborative heels which come with transparent glass heels. While the singer’s look is expensive and screams billionaire fashion, it is possible to imitate the look for one-tenth of its price tag.

Long overcoats with classic beige and brownish color palettes have been staples for fashionistas. Fans can find something similar to what T. Swizzle wore from Ardene for under $40 in the current sale.

Another slightly higher-end option for Tay’s coat dupe is Saks Fifth Avenue’s Raquel Check Double-Breasted Jacket which comes at $390 currently.

Reputation era is on the way

After the much-anticipated release of Taylor Swift’s 1989 Taylor’s Version, fans can’t wait to hear Reputation’s Taylor Version, and the Grammy-winning celebrity‘s new look is hinting that the era is not too far away.

The singer has also hinted at the Reputation era in her new Time interview, teasing that vaults tracks will be fire.

“It’s a goth-punk moment of female rage at being gaslit by an entire social structure. I think a lot of people see it and they’re just like, sick snakes and strobe lights.”

We say, bring it on. Taylor nation is ready.
