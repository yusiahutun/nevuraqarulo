Sarena Moore's dog stayed with her after Oregon college shooting

July 05, 2024
blog

Sarena Moore’s faithful service dog remained by her side even as she was shot dead by a depraved gunman in Oregon.

The mother-of-three was sitting in her wheelchair with her beloved dog Bullet beside her when Chris Harper-Mercer burst into her classroom at Umpqua Community College and opened fire.

On the morning of October 1, Bullet lay docile – as he was trained – when Harper-Mercer killed the 44-year-old, who was one of his first victims, as well as eight others.

Sarena Moore, a 44-year-old mother-of-three who used a wheelchair because of sciatica in her back, was among nine people killed by Chris Harper-Mercer during the Oregon school shooting last week

Her service dog Bullet (pictured) stayed by her side in her final moments and was handed back to her grieving family and loved ones, who said it was like getting a piece of her back

Bullet stayed with his owner in her final moments. Wherever Sarena went, Bullet went too – ready to help in case she ever struggled with her disability.

But he is not the same after that day, according to Sarena’s fiancé Travis Dow.

Dow, 46, who was due to marry Sarena in January, told CNN: ‘That’s what he is trained to do. He’s trained to stay right next to her no matter what.

‘You name it – he did everything with her.’

Dow added: ‘He’s been acting really droopy. He knows she ain’t coming home ‘cause he was there when the fatal day happened.’ 

When police handed Bullet, along with Sarena's wheelchair, back to her distraught fiance and family, they said it was like getting a piece of her back.

Dow told the New York Daily News: 'I got a piece of her with the dog. Plus I got a lot of her in here,' he said, motioning to his heart.

Always ready to assist in case she struggled with her disability, Bullet was by Sarena's side as she was shot dead last week at Umpqua Community College

According to witnesses, Mercer ordered Sarena to get on the floor after he burst into her classroom before ordering her back into her wheelchair, shooting her dead as she struggled

Sarena was among nine students and staff of Umpqua Community College who were killed by Harper-Mercer as he stormed on to the campus before opening fire.

While no exact motive has been revealed, police say that in a manifesto they recovered from the scene Harper-Mercer talks about being angry over not having a girlfriend.

Witnesses say that after the killer burst into their classroom, he ordered everyone, including Sarena, to lie down on the floor.

One account says Harper-Mercer toyed with her, ordering her to get back in the chair and watching her struggle before finally shooting and killing her.

Sarena's family today said she was a divorced mother of three adult children who had decided to go back to school to get a business degree.

According to Dow, she considered the opportunity a blessing from God, and had intended to use the qualification to open a stables to teach disabled children to ride because she loved horses. 

‘She wanted people to know there is better therapy out there than just dogs,’ he added.

Sarena's family also revealed that she suffered from sciatica in her back, which had grown progressively worse, making her wheelchair bound two years ago.

Mercer (his apartment pictured) appeared to have singled out his victims based on their religion, and Sarena was a devout Seventh Day Adventist, considering her place at college a blessing from God

Speaking of the shooting, Dow added: 'She was completely helpless, she could hurt nobody. She was the love of my life and there’s nothing in the world she could have done wrong.'

Cops also delivered her wheelchair, with Pastor Christian Martin of the Grants Pass Seventh Day Adventist church, where Sarena was an avid member, saying her son was 'shaken' by it, commenting on the fact that there were no bullet holes in it.

Following the shooting last week, Mercer turned a gun on himself, leaving police only with his manifesto to analyse his motives.

According to a source familiar with the document, which is reported to be a couple of pages long, Mercer believed everyone else was crazy and he was 'the only sane one'.

The source added that Mercer also discussed his hatred of black people, women and especially organized religion, scrawling the number 666 beside his other ramblings.

A source familiar with the document told People Magazine: 'The guy did this for satanic reasons. He did it to become a god in hell.'
