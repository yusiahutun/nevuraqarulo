1

American Girl creator Pleasant Rowland reinvigorated the town.

Jennifer Nalewicki

Nestled on the banks of Cayuga Lake in upstate New York, Aurora looks like it could have been ripped from the pages of a storybook. Less than a decade ago, the hamlet was just another blip on the map between New York City and Rochester, but thanks to a massive, village-wide restoration project led by Pleasant Rowland, who attended nearby Wells College, many of its grandest estates and coziest cottages have been transformed into charming accommodations, shops, and restaurants. With the recent opening of Wallcourt Hall, a 17-guest-room inn that once served as an all-girls preparatory school, Aurora is embarking on the next chapter of its story.

2

Many of its buildings are on the National Register of Historic Places.

Courtesy of Inns of Aurora

One such landmark is the E. B. Morgan House, built in 1858 for Colonel Edwin Barber Morgan and his family. In 2005, the Inns of Aurora restored the property and infused it with lavish touches like marble bathrooms and modern art. Today the house features seven guest rooms and an innkeeper who is available 24/7.

3

The Aurora Inn was once a stagecoach stop for the Wells Fargo Express.

Courtesy of Inns of Aurora

Weary travelers going by stagecoach would often overnight at this Federal-style, columned building built in 1838. The original stagecoach still exists and is used at commencement ceremonies at Wells College, which was established by Henry Wells, who also founded American Express.

4

The Inn is still a popular stop for travelers—for different reasons.

Courtesy of Inns of Aurora

Today the fully restored Aurora Inn features 10 guest rooms, some of which have private porches and fireplaces, plus an onsite restaurant with lake views.

5

It's part of the Finger Lakes wine region.

Jennifer Nalewicki

The Finger Lakes area has been on the radar of wine enthusiasts for quite some time; one particular venue that has critics talking is Heart & Hands Wine Company, run by husband-and-wife team Tom and Susan Higgins. Take a walk through the winery's vertical vineyards or sample a Riesling or Pinot Noir at the onsite tasting room. Hearts & Hands is the closest winery to Aurora, six miles north in Union Springs.

6

MacKenzie-Childs' iconic ceramics are handmade in Aurora.

Courtesy of MacKenzie Childs

One of the world's leading pottery manufacturers, MacKenzie-Childs employs a team of artisans who craft every piece on-site, from molding clay into mugs and saucers to applying intricate paint patterns in a kaleidoscope of colors.

7

You can visit the pottery maker's campus.

Courtesy of MacKenzie-Childs

MacKenzie-Childs artists, who count the beauty of nature among their inspirations, work their magic at the brand's facilities, its headquarters since 1983.

8

MacKenzie-Childs even has its own farmhouse.

Courtesy of MacKenzie-Childs

The property features a farmhouse decorated from top to bottom in MacKenzie-Childs ceramics and decor, from the rugs and furniture to the walls and curtains. No two rooms are decorated the same, and some include discontinued pieces from when the company got its start.

9

There's an inn named after the American Girl founder.

Courtesy of the Inns of Aurora

Another historical building in town is Rowland House, a Queen Anne-style mansion with a wrap-around porch and 10 guest rooms. Rowland had a hand in decorating the interiors, including several guest rooms that were in collaboration with MacKenzie-Childs. Return guests often request specific rooms, with the most popular being the one decked out in black-and-white "Courtly Checks," a signature MacKenzie-Childs pattern.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

10

The Morgan Opera House once served as a community library and jail.

Jennifer Nalewicki

When Louise Morgan Zabriskie commissioned the building of the Tudor-style Morgan Opera House, she envisioned it as a community center with spaces to house the town hall, a library, live performances by local and traveling acts, and a jail cell. Today the 117-year-old building hosts concerts and other live performances throughout the year.

11

Dorie's Bakery is a stop on the Finger Lakes Sweet Treat Trail.

Courtesy of Inns of Aurora

Cookies, loaves of bread, and cupcakes are just some of the goodies baked onsite at Dorie's Bakery, which is part of the Finger Lakes Sweet Treat Trail. In the evening, the entire operation flips over into a pizzeria pop-up replete with red-and-white checkerboard tablecloths, and serves hand-tossed pizzas and homemade cannoli. Prefer a pint of beer and a game of pool instead? Then head across the street to Fargo Bar & Grill—in Aurora, nearly everything is within walking distance.
