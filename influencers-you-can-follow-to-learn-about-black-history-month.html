Influencers You Can Follow to Learn About Black History Month

January 30, 2024
blog

It’s currently Black History Month in the United States. To celebrate this month, we’ve listed 5 influencers you can follow to learn more about it. 

What Is Black History Month?

Before we move on to our list of influencers you can follow to learn about Black History Month, it is nice to gain some insight as to what Black History Month is. 

Black History Month is an annual celebration of African Americans’ achievements and an opportunity to recognize their pivotal role in American history. The celebration, which is also known as African American History Month, evolved from “Negro History Week,” which was created by acclaimed historian Carter G. Woodson and other important African Americans. Since 1976, every president of the United States has declared February to be Black History Month. Other countries, including Canada and the United Kingdom, have also dedicated a month to honoring Black history. 

Influencers Who Talk about Black History Month

If you’re interested in learning more about Black History Month, check out some of the influencers that are actively addressing it on social media.

Glo Atanmo

Glo Atanmo, a Nigerian-American entrepreneur, is the creator of @glographics, an Instagram account dedicated to anti-racism, wellness, and cultural commentary. She educates her audience with clear, contemporary visuals. Glo, as a businesswoman, understands the value of branding, and in Black History Month, she offers guidance to businesses and brands interested in addressing the issue. Currently, she has more than 250K followers on Instagram.

Blair Imani

Blair Imani is an educational influencer who covers a wide range of themes, including race, gender, feminism, alcoholism, diet culture, and a variety of other sensitive subjects. The Smarter in Seconds series is a highlight among Blair’s content. She offers fast answers to a lot of challenging topics using Instagram Reel’s 60-second time limit. Her Instagram follower count is over 450K. 

Ibram X. Kendi

Ibram X. Kendi is the head of the Boston University Center for Antiracist Research, as well as an author. Ibram is a key figure in the study of African-American history and culture, and his book, Four Hundred Souls, debuted at the top of the New York Times nonfiction bestseller list. Ibram presently has 1.1 million Instagram followers. He is one of the most popular Instagram influencers in 2022, and his audience continues to grow.

Rachel Cargle

Rachel Cargle is an anti-racism activist, author, and scholar based in New York City. She uses her Instagram account to educate her followers. Rachel introduces a new topic each day, which we must investigate on our own. She currently has 1.17 million followers on the platform. 

Ijeoma Oluo

After George Floyd’s death, Ijeoma Oluo, who has been writing and speaking on race for years, witnessed an increased interest in her work. Her New York Times best-seller book So You Want to Talk About Race (2018), has put her onto must-read lists. She now has over 500K followers on Instagram.
