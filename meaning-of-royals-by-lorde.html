Meaning of Royals by Lorde

August 30, 2024
blog

In Lorde's hit song "Royals," the overarching theme explores societal pressure and the rejection of materialism. The lyrics offer a critique of the excessive luxury and wealth often idolized in popular culture. Through cleverly crafted lyrics, Lorde paints a picture of a different kind of aspiration, one focusing on personal fulfillment, self-acceptance, and contentment.One standout lyric that illustrates this theme is "I've never seen a diamond in the flesh, I cut my teeth on wedding rings in the movies." Here, Lorde challenges the traditional symbol of wealth and status. She suggests that her experiences and influences have not been shaped by material possessions but rather by Hollywood's portrayal of love and romance. This alludes to the idea that personal connections and emotional depth are more important than material wealth.Another notable lyric that reinforces the theme is "But every song's like gold teeth, Grey Goose, trippin' in the bathroom, bloodstains, ball gowns, trashin' the hotel room." This lyric satirizes the glamorization of excessive partying and extravagant lifestyles prevalent in popular culture. Lorde highlights the emptiness and superficiality of these pursuits, suggesting that genuine happiness cannot be found in material excess, but rather through more meaningful experiences and connections.In the chorus, the repetition of "And we'll never be royals" emphasizes the rejection of societal expectations and the notion that not all dreams revolve around wealth and luxury. Lorde asserts that it is perfectly fine to not conform to the standard definition of success, urging listeners to find their own path and prioritize their own version of happiness.The lyric "Let me be your ruler, you can call me queen bee, and baby I'll rule" adds a layer of empowerment to the song. Lorde encourages listeners to embrace their individuality and take control of their destinies. She suggests that true power lies in self-acceptance and self-expression, rather than in conforming to societal norms or seeking approval from others.Lastly, the bridge lyrics "We're bigger than we ever dreamed, and I'm in love with being queen" express a sense of confidence and self-assurance. Lorde celebrates the realization that one's worth does not depend on material possessions or external validation. Instead, true fulfillment comes from embracing one's unique qualities and finding joy in the journey towards self-discovery and personal growth.In conclusion, Lorde's "Royals" presents a powerful and thought-provoking message about rejecting societal pressure and finding fulfillment outside of materialism. Through intricate and imaginative lyrics, the song encourages listeners to define their own version of success and to find happiness in authenticity, self-acceptance, and meaningful connections. It stands as a reminder that true wealth and power come from within, rather than through the accumulation of material possessions.
