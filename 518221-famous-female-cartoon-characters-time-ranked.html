30 famous female cartoon characters of all time, ranked

May 27, 2024
blog

Female cartoon characters are not only animated figures on the screen; they're smart, beautiful, brave and iconic heroines that many girls love to watch and relate to. Besides entertainment, these animated characters have inspired audiences across generations. But which characters are the most famous ones?

There has been a great revolution in the characterization of female cartoons. It's not all about fairy tale characters. Girls and people of all ages can relate to these characters.

Most popular female cartoon characters of all time

Female cartoon characters have captivated audiences for generations, leaving a lasting impact. Ranking them may bring back memories, spark debates, and celebrate the lasting legacy of these animated wonders. Here is a list of the iconic female cartoon characters:

1. Wonder Woman (DC Comics and various animated adaptations)

Who is the most powerful female cartoon character? Meet Wonder Woman, often depicted as a tall, athletic woman with long, dark hair. Wonder Woman wears a golden tiara on her forehead. She uses her indestructible bracelets and Lasso of Truth to fight for justice.

2. Princess Jasmine (Disney's Aladdin)

What is the most famous girl cartoon? Jasmine is. She has expressive brown eyes and beautiful Arabian-inspired attire. However, her independent and adventurous spirit, as she defies traditional expectations of royalty and her loyal tiger Rajah, has made her a beloved Disney princess.

3. Red Sonja (Marvel Comics and animated adaptations)

Red Sonja stands out, and she has been many people's favourite. Characterized by her striking red hair, Red Sonja is a fierce warrior who navigates a dangerous world, seeking justice and revenge. Her boldness has made her a popular character in the fantasy genre.

4. Kim Possible (Disney's Kim Possible)

Kim is one of the hottest female cartoon characters, often depicted in her signature spy-inspired outfit of a black crop top, green cargo pants, boots, a black utility belt, and gloves. She is a teenage spy who balances high school life with saving the world from various villains.

5. Daphne Blake (Hanna-Barbera's Scooby-Doo series)

She is a member of the Mystery Inc. gang in the Scooby-Doo series. Daphne is known for her fashionable outfits, red hair, and penchant for danger. But her bravery, curiosity, and determination to solve crimes have made her a fan favourite.

6. Megara (Disney's Hercules)

Megara, alias Meg, is a strong heroine who is a memorable character due to her sarcastic wit and independent nature. Her journey from a troubled past to finding love again showcases her resilience.

7. Ariel (Disney's The Little Mermaid)

Who doesn't love Ariel, the curious and adventurous mermaid who captivates audiences with her vibrant red hair and desire to explore the human world? Known for her enchanting voice and determination to follow her dreams, Ariel portrays the importance of staying true to oneself.

8. Gwen Stacy (Marvel Comics)

Gwen Stacy is known as Peter Parker's love interest in the Spider-Man storyline. She is an intelligent and compassionate character, making her an enduring figure in the Spider-Man mythos.

9. Queen Elsa (Disney's Frozen)

Queen Elsa is a character in an iconic blue dress, with blonde hair and blue eyes, and possesses magical ice powers that she struggles to control. With the anthem Let It Go, Elsa represents the journey of self-acceptance. Her story of sisterly love has resonated with audiences worldwide.

10. Korra (Nickelodeon's The Legend of Korra)

Korra is one of the most famous brunette cartoon characters. She is the protagonist of the animated series, and as the current incarnation of the Avatar, Korra possesses the power to manipulate all four elements. Her bravery throughout the series makes her a beloved character.

11. Moana (Disney's Moana)

Moana, the adventurous Polynesian princess with curly black hair, sets sail to save her people and restore the heart of Te Fiti. She is a strong-willed character who, despite her parents keeping her away from the ocean, she connects with it. She showcases bravery and a deep sense of identity rooted in her culture.

12. Scarlet Witch (Marvel Comics)

Scarlet Witch, alias Wanda Maximoff, possesses reality-altering powers as a prominent character in Marvel Comics. Her journey explores themes of power and identity.

13. Rapunzel (Disney's Tangled)

Rapunzel is an adventurous princess who is kept unaware of her royal heritage by a witch, Mother Gothel. However, she escapes from the secluded tower using her long magical hair to explore the outside world. Rapunzel's journey is full of self-discovery and will surely inspire you.

14. La Belle Enfant (Disney's Beauty and the Beast)

Voiced by Paige O'Hara, Belle is a beautiful and kind character who sees beauty where others don't. She is a confident red-haired brunette who knows that you shouldn't judge a book by its cover, in this case, a beast by its appearance. She teaches us the importance of unconditional love.

15. Tinker Bell (Disney's Peter Pan and the Disney Fairies franchise)

Tinker Bell, the mischievous Disney toon fairy with blonde hair, originated from J.M. Barrie's Peter Pan. She is a sassy fairy who has become one of the most popular Disney iconic characters.

16. Fa Mulan (Disney's Mulan)

In a male-chauvinistic society, Mulan is a female character who appears courageous, and her determination sets her apart. Disguised as a man, Mulan takes her father's place in the Chinese army to fight against an invading force.

Known for her bravery and martial arts skills, Mulan breaks gender stereotypes and proves her worth on the battlefield.

17. Lois Griffin (TV show Family Guy)

As the wife of Peter Griffin and mother of three children, Lois is known for her reasoning and resilience as she balances her family life with her ambitions and challenges.

18. Sonya Blade (Mortal Kombat franchise)

Sonya Blade is a skilled fighter. As a member of the Special Forces, she uses her martial arts expertise to combat the forces of evil.

19. Psylocke (Marvel Comics)

Psylocke, alias Elizabeth Braddock, is a mutant superheroine who uses her telepathic, telekinetic abilities and martial arts skills to fight evil. Alongside the X-Men, she protects mutants and humanity. She's known for her psychic katana and signature purple hair.

20. Chel (DreamWorks' The Road to El Dorado)

With her street smarts, Chel becomes a key ally to the film's protagonists, Miguel and Tulio, as they search for the legendary city of gold. Chel is known for her wit, beauty, and level mind in precarious situations.

21. Raven (DC Comics and animated adaptations)

Raven in Teen Titans DC Comics possesses mystical powers. She struggles to keep her emotions in check while battling evil forces but finally conquers the struggle portraying her inner strength.

22. Emma Frost (Marvel Comics)

Emma Frost is a powerful mutant often associated with the X-Men. Her ability to transform into diamond form and her telepathic abilities makes her a formidable ally.

23. Kidagakash Nedakh (Disney's Atlantis: The Lost Empire)

Kidagakash Nedakh, or Kida, is a Disney princess and the daughter of the Atlantean king. She possesses the Heart of Atlantis and helps the film's protagonist to unravel the mysteries of her ancient civilization. Kida is known for her strength, wisdom, and connection to the lost city.

24. Katara (Nickelodeon's Avatar: The Last Airbender)

As a member of Team Avatar, Katara uses her water-bending skills to fight against the Fire Nation's oppression. The character is powerful and influential, and her beauty will grab your attention.

25. Rogue (Marvel Comics)

Rogue, alias Anna Marie, is a mutant superheroine from Marvel Comics. With her ability to absorb the powers and memories of others through touch, Rogue struggles with her powers' consequences. However, her journey towards self-acceptance is inspiring to many people.

26. Madeline Fenton (TV show Danny Phantom)

Madeline Fenton from Danny Phantom is a brilliant scientist and ghost hunter who plays an essential role in her son's adventures as he fights against supernatural threats. Maddie's dedication to protecting her family and the world from ghosts depicts the strength of women.

27. Esmeralda (Disney's The Hunchback of Notre Dame)

This Romani woman faces discrimination as she fights for justice in medieval Paris. Esmeralda is a fan's favourite because of her free-spirited nature, fiery personality, and iconic song God Help the Outcasts.

28. Striparella (Adult Swim's Stripperella)

Striparella is the alter ego of Jones, a lap dancer by day and superheroine by night. With her charm and crime-fighting abilities, she takes on villains and saves the day. She brings a unique blend of adult themes and superhero tropes, making the animation worthy to watch.

29. Medusa (Marvel Comics)

Medusalith Amaquelin Boltagon is a member of the Inhuman royal family in Marvel Comics. She has prehensile hair that has incredible strength. She is a formidable warrior and leader known for her regal presence, fierce loyalty, and determination to protect the inhuman civilization.

30. Starfire (DC Comics)

Starfire, or Princess Koriand'r, is a prominent character in DC Comics, most notably in Teen Titans. Hailing from the planet Tamaran, Starfire will entice you with her power to fly, optimism, and loyalty to her friends. Starfire embodies compassion and her love across the cosmos.
