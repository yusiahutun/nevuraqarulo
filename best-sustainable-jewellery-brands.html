Our favourite sustainable jewellery brands

August 20, 2024
blog

Beabond

SHOP NOW

Inspired by its founder Ferelith Moltke’s time spent living in Botswana, Beabond uses entirely traceable, carbon-neutral diamonds and recycled gold to create its collections, whilst supporting biodiversity initiatives across Africa. The brand regularly collaborates with charities that align with its mission – most recently teaming up with the supermodel and climate activist Arizona Muse’s charity DIRT to launch a series of glorious lockets depicting the endangered coco de mer nut, from which 30 per cent of proceeds will go towards the organisation’s work in soil regeneration to combat climate change.

Completedworks

SHOP NOW

Completedworks specialises in creating contemporary jewellery – as well as beautiful ceramics – from responsibly sourced materials. Handcrafted in London, its founder Anna Jewsbury was inspired by reductionism after studying Mathematics & Philosophy at Oxford, leading to her design process of drawing contrasts between high-quality materials and purist elements. The label is loved and worn by the most stylish women, including Ashley Graham, Adwoa Aboah, Jodie Comer, Naomie Harris, Emma Watson and Maya Rudolph.

Elhanati

SHOP NOW

Elhanati is a Danish jewellery brand which draws inspiration from Nordic minimalistic lines and the mysterious energy and surroundings of the Middle East. Each piece is handcrafted in Copenhagen using 18ct solid gold, and – thanks to the traditional techniques used in the process – no two pieces are ever the same. As well as using a high proportion of recycled metals and ethically-sourced stones, the brand does all manufacturing locally to reduce its carbon footprint.

Lark and Berry

SHOP LARK AND BERRY

Few would argue against the allure of diamonds, but sourcing these precious stones is blighted with ethical issues - from environmental devastation to the exploitation of workers. Lark and Berry offer sustainably-sourced, cultured diamonds, which are grown using lab technology. They're of the same quality as mined diamonds, but with the added bonus of sustainable credentials.

Vrai

SHOP VRAI

Fine jewellery brand Vrai ('true' in French), specialises in modern, elegant jewellery that's designed to be worn forever. The diamonds are sustainably produced in America with no carbon footprint and no mining, set in recycled gold, and polished to the highest international standards by their in-house craftsmen. The jewellery brand is regularly worn on the red carpet, with Jennifer Lopez, Reese Witherspoon and Angelina Jolie being fans.

Ysso

SHOP NOW

Ysso is a London-based, demi-fine jewellery brand that was founded by Greek mother-and-daughter duo Stalo and Alexia Karides. Stocked online, the designs are a modern tribute to a traditional Greek aesthetic, embracing the forms and structure of Mediterranean art, architecture and archaeology. The brand (which is loved by the likes of Hailey Bieber and Nicola Coughlan) has a number of sustainable credentials: all of their designs are made by hand in recycled bronze and gold in a small family-run foundry and workshop in Greece. They are always produced in small quantities, almost always under 10 for any design, and are non-seasonal.

Lylie

SHOP NOW

Lylie is one of the only brands in the world that sources its designs from urban mining; everything is made from salvaged electronic waste (discarded phones, tablets and computers), dental waste and lab-grown or recycled, antique diamonds. Such is the extent of her pioneering efforts to lower her brand’s carbon footprint, the brand's founder Eliza Walter has been awarded a special hallmark by the London Assay Office.

Gemfields

SHOP GEMFIELDS

Gemfields is one of the leading responsibly sourced jewellers, and one which specialises in colourful and unique pieces to treasure for a lifetime. "We seek to do things differently and to maintain a considered balance between people, profit and the environment," reads its mission statement. The jeweller remains responsible by adhering to three main pillars of legitimacy, transparency and integrity (read more on its sustainability practices, here).

Jessie Thomas

SHOP JESSIE THOMAS

Jessie Thomas' pieces are distinctive in their unfussy simplicity - a style signature seen throughout her collections. Inspired by her father, jeweller David Thomas - with whom she shares a workshop in Pimlico - Jessie set out to create subtle, wearable pieces that transcend trends, often featuring pearls and hammered gold. She hand-makes all the jewellery herself, meaning that each of her pieces is truly one-of-a-kind, and all the designs are created using 100 per cent recycled gold and sustainably sourced diamonds.

Loveness Lee

SHOP LOVENESS LEE

As Loveness Lee designs are inspired by nature, the brand works hard to protect it. All the designs are handmade in London, cast in Scotland and packaged in biodegradable and recyclable materials. Loveness Lee is perfect for those who are after unique statement pieces which are sustainable and are a friendly price point. The brand views jewellery as art, which is clear from its collections, made using silicone moulding techniques that achieve intricate patterns and beautiful formations.

Alexandra Hakim

SHOP ALEXANDRA HAKIM

Alexandra Hakim gives a new lease of life to materials and objects which would otherwise go to waste. The brand takes inspiration from anywhere, whether it's from tomato stems from bustling food markets to used matchsticks found at home which are then repurposed into contemporary pieces of jewellery.

Each of Alexandra Hakim's pieces is meticulously made by hand, completely unique and naturally zero-waste. Her signature method involves casting found objects and used materials directly into precious metals so that nothing is wasted and each piece is completely unique.

Ingle & Rhode

SHOP INGLE & RHODE

Ingle & Rhode offers engagement and wedding rings that are safe from conflict. It all began back in 2006 when co-founder David Rhode was hunting for an engagement ring and struggled to locate a jeweller who was able to tell him exactly where the diamonds came from or how they were produced. He then partnered with his friend Tim Ingle to offer an ethical alternative."When it comes to engagement rings and fine jewellery, diamonds are particularly problematic," he told us. "Blood diamonds are mined in war-torn countries where conflict is often funded by the diamond trade. Here, diamonds signify worker exploitation and atrocious human rights abuses rather than romance and commitment."

Daphine

SHOP DAPHINE

"All of our pieces are handmade in our atelier in Jaipur – something we are very transparent about as we believe it's important for people to know where their jewellery is coming from," Daphine co-founder Damasia Ball told us. The brand's designers work with local artisans on only a few key designs in small quantities, meaning that they sell everything they produce, reducing leftover waste. Daphine treats jewellery with the idea that it reflects personal taste and won't go out of style in a matter of months. "We love pieces similar to the ones we grew up seeing our mothers and grandmothers wear," Ball explained. "Classic, timeless icons."

Laura Lombardi

SHOP NOW

Brooklyn-based designer Laura Lombardi creates minimalist jewellery that can be part of your everyday look. Each piece is handcrafted from a mix of new, recycled and reclaimed metals that she finds on her travels. Her brand has a strong celebrity fanbase, with everyone from Emma Watson to Tracee Ellis Ross wearing her designs. Most of her pieces are made in New York, where she works sustainably with a Brooklyn-based brass mill, and she is one of the few jewellery designers to feature on Net-a-Porter's Net Sustain jewellery section.

Edge of Ember

SHOP NOW

Edge of Ember's mission is to create timeless, fuss-free pieces of jewellery designed to be worn every day. A little slice of affordable luxury, the dainty, desirable pieces are all made from sterling silver, solid gold and semi-precious stones.

"We believe that fashion should look and feel good," they explain on their website. "That is why ethical production and honest social responsibility are the foundations on which we built our brand. We work with artisan groups and small-scale factories in Asia that are run in an ethical manner, that employ craftsmen skilled in their trade."

Leigh Miller

SHOP NOW

A must-know name for anyone who loves sculptural jewellery, LA-based designer Leigh Miller launched her collection in 2014, after working for J.Crew and Calvin Klein. Her work stands out as she uses an ancient tradition of lost-wax casting to create her unique pieces, which are designed and crafted locally in LA. Sustainability is key to Miller and the brand, as she makes a conscious effort to use recycled silver and conflict-free stones – and she counts Michelle Obama as a fan.

Pippa Small

SHOP PIPPA SMALL

Canadian-born jewellery designer Pippa Small has established a strong following with her striking organic necklaces, rings and earrings. Each covetable piece is designed in London and crafted in Rajasthan, India, by a team of highly skilled goldsmiths and stonecutters.

Wwake

SHOP WWAKE

Wwake's designs are easily spotted, defined by their signature contoured shapes and unusual stone arrangements. Each design uses recycled and fair-mined gold and non-conflict diamonds to make its jewellery, which is locally produced in New York. The brand has been worn by celebrities such as Rihanna and Cate Blanchett.

Catbird

SHOP CATBIRD

Designers Rony Vardi and Leigh Plessner's brand Catbird has established a cult A-list following, including the likes of Emma Watson and Michelle Williams. With more than 100 artisan employers in their Brooklyn studio, each piece of jewellery is designed and crafted by hand. The label, which is part of Net-a-Porter's Net Sustain edit, supports the living wage and its local community by contributing 1 per cent of its annual sales to nearby charities.

Kimberly McDonald

SHOP KIMBERLY MCDONALD

"I like to think of each creation as an extension of what nature has given us," explains Kimberly McDonald whose company works with a number of environmental charities. She seeks out the finest natural materials, reclaimed gold, and recycled diamonds to handcraft her collection in the United States with sustainability in mind.
