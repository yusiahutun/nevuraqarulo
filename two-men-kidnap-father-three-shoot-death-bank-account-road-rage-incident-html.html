Two men kidnap Idaho father and shoot him to death before emptying his bank account

July 21, 2024
blog

A father-of-three was kidnapped by two men and shot to death after a road rage incident in quiet Coeur d'Alene, Idaho. 

X-ray technician William 'Bo' Kirk, 41, got into an altercation with two men while driving home from work on October 22. 

The men then followed Kirk home and kidnapped him just as he was getting out of his car in his own driveway.

Investigators then believe that two men took him to an area in Hayden Creek, near a shooting range, where they proceeded to bound Kirk and shoot him to death. His body was found three days later.  

On Friday, October 28, police arrested two men for Kirk's murder. David Hutto, 44, was arrested at a Burger King and has since been arraigned on a second-degree murder charge. His alleged accomplice has not been named because he is in federal custody on a related charge and his name likely won't be released until next week.

The two are believed to be the men caught on surveillance cameras driving Kirk's truck after his murder, emptying the man's bank account and buying gasoline at a gas station to torch the vehicle. 

In court on Monday, Hutto appeared over a live video feed and seemed unemotional as prosecutors laid out the details of the case. 

'Mr. Kirk was followed home after the defendant was enraged by something Mr. Kirk did,' Deputy Prosecuting Attorney Rebecca Perez said in Kootenai County Court.

'This appears to be a random attack - deliberate and brutal,' Perez continued. 'Following the murder, the defendant took measures to hide what had happened. Purchased some gasoline, burned Mr. Kirk's car. Further victimized Mr. Kirk's family using Mr. Kirk's credit cards.'

Kirk's sister, Claudia Jackson, was in the court room for Monday's arraignment. She told KHQ-TV that Hutto looked 'smug' as the charges were laid out.

Jackson says that her brother was well loved. He is survived by his wife Amanda and their three children. He was also about to become a grandfather.

'He loved his family and friends,' Jackson told CDAPress.com. 'If he was not at work or golfing, he was with family and friends. He did whatever he could to make you laugh and had nicknames for a lot of people.'

The judge set Hutto's bond at $2million. He is due back in court sometime in the next two weeks for a hearing. 

Kirk is survived by his wife Amanda and their three children. He was about to become a grandfather 

At the time of his arrest, Hutto had warrants out in other states. He also has an extensive criminal history including charges of theft and robbery. 

The investigation into Kirk's death is ongoing. Police are currently seeking to speak with possible witnesses who were driving a red car near the area of the shooting on October 22 between 7 and 8pm. They are being asked to contact the detective division of the Kootenai County Sheriff's Office at 208-446-1300. 

Meanwhile, Kirk's family have organized a memorial for him to be held this Saturday at 3pm at the Quail Run Ranch. A benefit account has also been set up for him at branches of the Spokane Teachers Credit Union.
