ATP Moselle Open: Ugo Humbert vs Alexander Shevchenko; Preview, Head-To-Head andPrediction

September 17, 2024
blog

The ATP Moselle Open is about to come to its conclusion. It served as a bridge to tennis fans as they were able to witness lesser-known players after the Paris Masters. Now, the tournament will see Ugo Humbert facing Alexander Shevchenko. Both players have come into the final to win the championship, and they will lay it all out on the court.

As soon as the Moselle Open ends, the Nitto ATP Finals will begin. Before fans see the top talent of tennis battle it out in Turin, they will see one ultimate player crowned as champion in France.

Ugo Humbert vs Alexander Shevchenko: Preview

Humbert is a 25-year-old French player who is steadily rising up the ATP ladder. He has participated in all Slams this year and some of the major Masters this year. He attained his best records in both. His best in Slams was a Round 3 appearance in the Australian Open. He reached the quarterfinal in the Shanghai Masters, his best result so far. He also won two clay Challenger tournaments this year in May. 

Coming into the final of the Moselle Open, Humbert defeated Dominic Thiem in round 2 and Fabio Fognini in the semifinal. He has only dropped one set, which he fought hard to win. Now, he awaits one of his biggest challenges in the tournament.

On the other side, Shevchenko has also moved up the rankings by a huge margin this season. He achieved a career-high of 63 in October. Despite climbing higher, Shevchenko has not managed to win any ATP tournaments this year. However, he has won two challenger tournaments in Bratislava and Madrid. He performed great in the Swiss Indoors, where he beat Stan Wawrinka and Taylor Fritz after coming up from the qualifiers.

In the Moselle Open, he has only dropped one set till the final. He will now face Humbert as his ultimate battle, hoping to win his first ATP 250 tournament.

Humbert vs Shevchenko: Head-to-Head

Humbert and Shevchenko have never faced each other before. The two finalists will play against each other for the first time in Metz. Their bout will be a surprise for the audience in France, who are more likely to support the local player. Both players will exchange rallies to make sure that they are the ones who will have a better chance of being the victor.

Prediction: Humbert to take the championship 

The bout between both players will show us some great points. Shevchenko has moved up the ATP ladder, but his run in the Moselle Open will end with him being the runner-up of the tournament.
