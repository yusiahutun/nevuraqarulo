Update, 8/30, 11:30 PM: Controversial VMAs host Miley Cyrus had a minor wardrobe malfunction during tonight's broadcast; she accidentally flashed her nipple while prepping for her closing performance. Surprisingly, wasn't the most shocking moment from the evening.

Update, 8/30: How is VMAs host Miley Cyrus prepping for tonight's ceremony? With a naked Instagram, of course. Cyrus shared a risqué selfie of her naked body on Instagram ahead of tonight's ceremony. We can only imagine what tonight's show will bring.

Update, 8/29: First, the Parents Television Council was concerned about Miley Cyrus hosting the 2015 Video Music Awards, but now even MTV is worried? Well, kind of. 

Cyrus, known for her wild ways, was an obvious choice to host the ever-controversial MTV Video Music Awards. But the pop star's expectedly unexpected antics might still be censored.

"We have [planned] a lot of wide camera shots and a lot of audience reaction shows, VMAs executive producer Van Toffler tells Billboard. "And we have a delay so we're prepared for her."

While her penchant for being provocative will surely be celebrated tomorrow night, Toffler remains cautious—because he knows what kind of controversy the "We Can't Stop" singer is capable of. After all, he did produce Cyrus' 2013 twerk-a-thon heard 'round the world. You know, the one with Robin Thicke and that foam finger.

"There's a chance for anything," he explains. "She feels like the perfect host. She's that great marriage of music credibility, candor, she uses social media so well to express herself and she's provocative and chaotic and unpredictable, everything we want for this show."

Update, 8/28: Nicki Minaj will be opening  this Sunday's 2015 MTV Video Music Awards. The "Anaconda" singer made the announcement by sharing a sonogram photo featuring a baby MTV moonman, poking fun at recent pregnancy rumors surrounding she and fiance Meek Mill. Minaj later deleted the tweet, but MTV confirmed she is still set to perform at the show's kick off. 

Media Platforms Design Team

Things could get a little awkward as yesterday, Miley Cyrus (who will be hosting the awards show), called out Minaj in an interview with the New York Times for her "not so polite" tweets after not being nominated for Video of the Year—which led to a Twitter spat with Taylor Swift. Minaj hasn't yet responded to Cyrus's comments so we're sure things will be interesting to say the least. 

The MTV VMAs will air this Sunday live at 9PM. 

Update, 8/27: Ahead of Miley Cyrus's big hosting gig at this Sunday's MTV Video Music Awards, VMA executive producer Garrett English has revealed that the controversial 22-year-old singer will have "free rein" as the host. While the Parents Television Council has already expressed concern for Cyrus's hosting job, English went on to say: 

"Obviously there are standards and various other things that were always a part of it, but no, [no rules]… It never comes from a place of trying to limit the range, it's always coming from a place of trying to open up the full palate. Miley has been fantastic."

Cyrus has been teasing sneak-peeks at Sunday's show along with the star-studded seating chart which features Kylie Jenner and Britney Spears seated next to each other as well as a section for Taylor Swift's girl gang. 

Update, 8/21: Kanye West will join the ranks of Madonna, Beyoncé, Britney Spears and the King of Pop himself when he receives the Michael Jackson Video Vanguard Award at Sunday night's MTV Video Music Awards. While we wait to see if West and Kim Kardashian will recreate Beyoncé and Jay Z's onstage family moment from last year, watch a promo for the award below.
