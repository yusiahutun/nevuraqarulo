Live like a king! Five-story fairytale castle hits the market in Michigan for $2.3 MILLION

August 26, 2024
blog

A 'magnificent' faux fairytale castle that looks straight out of a Disney film with its secret passageways, 'dungeon,' and working drawbridge has hit the market in Michigan for $2,299,000. 

The 26-room, five-story fortress hidden in the woodlands of Rochester, Michigan caught the attention of social media users after photos emerged of its stunningly 'eccentric' structure and decor.

Estate agents Sotheby's International Realty listed it on property site Zillow three months ago, but they have yet to find the 'king or queen of the castle,' despite being bombarded by viewing requests.

Images show the 60-ton steel structure topped by medieval stone-walled turrets, and there's even a moat, portcullis, and drawbridge to protect its grand entrance.

Many of the 26 rooms are lined with oak walls and linked by wide staircases from the vast entrance hall, while the master bedroom appears fit for royalty with a four-poster bed and balcony.

Hidden within the walls of the property is a 50-foot-tall spiral staircase, which leads from the roof terrace to the secret dungeon-like cave where a Tudor-style bar and wine cellar can be found.

Photos also show elaborately done bedrooms — plus at least one suit of armor inside, guarding the home.

The stunning home was designed by James LeBlanc and completed in 1991 as an engagement present to his then-wife, Vera LeBlanc — 22 years after he promised to build her one because he couldn't afford a ring.

Realtor Mike Kuligowski believes the fortress, which has seven bathrooms, is perfect for an 'eccentric' buyer who dreamed of living in a castle as a child.

The 34-year-old, who specializes in selling unique homes, admits even he was blown away after thinking the most recent owner was exaggerating when he said he's 'selling a castle.'
