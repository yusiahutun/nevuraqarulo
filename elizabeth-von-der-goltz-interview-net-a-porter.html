As the global buying director of Net-a-Porter, Elizabeth von der Goltz is a woman who knows more than most about putting together a stylish wardrobe that will work season after season. And, following the launch of Jet-a-Porter – the holiday branch of the fashion retail site – her knowledge of vacation labels now knows no bounds.

So, from her tips for packing light and how to approach swimwear shopping to her favourite pieces to buy this summer and what she's learnt from her style mistakes, here is Elizabeth's definitive guide to holiday shopping and packing.

What would be your biggest tip for packing light?

"I put all of my bikinis and swimsuits into one big zip pouch and then I roll up all of my cover-ups and dresses so I can fit everything into one carry-on suitcase. I’m not a fan of putting anything into the hold."

How has your holiday style changed over the years?

"As I’ve been to more and more beach destinations over the years, I’ve kept on growing my holiday wardrobe, so I now tend to change my style depending on the location I’m in. Each new place inspires new ideas and different ways to style your favourite pieces."

Why do you think people are so much more interested in resort brands at the moment?

"Our customers are now travelling on average up to 11 times a year, so beachwear has become increasingly important. Over the past two years alone, we have seen a huge shift in our high-summer sales which have grown by over 30 per cent. But this shift doesn’t just mean swimwear, over 60 per cent of our beachwear sales come from ready-to-wear, e.g. dresses and cover ups, so we decided it was important to work with brands collaboratively to create exclusive products across all of our categories. We launched our vacation shop, Jet-a-Porter this year to be that one-stop shop for all our customers’ holiday needs, from her suitcase, sun hat and tote to her sunglasses and even her tanning products and sun cream."

How do you choose your swimwear?

"I like a simple low-cut bathing suit with a plunging neckline because it’s flattering and I have lots of bandeau bikinis as they’re the best for avoiding any visible tan lines. Or I love something fun like the double breasted Solid & Striped one-piece or the Gucci logo swimsuit, which feels a bit more eccentric."

What have been your biggest style mistakes with swimwear and what have you learnt from this?

"I now steer clear of any interesting straps and cut outs on my swimwear, as this gives the craziest tan lines that are then impossible to get rid of."

What would be your advice for helping your holiday wardrobe work season after season?

"Invest in beautiful cover-ups and kaftans and keep hold of them, as you’ll inevitably bring them out again for your next holiday. I still have ones I wear now that I bought years ago and it’s the same with triangle or bandeau bikinis. Invest in luxury beachwear because it never goes out of style."
