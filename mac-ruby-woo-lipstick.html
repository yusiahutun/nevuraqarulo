History of the Hero: MAC Ruby Woo Lipstick

August 22, 2024
blog

The classic red lipstick. All at once a symbol of glamour and rebellion, femininity and defiance. It incites feelings of joy but can also feel intimidating (in a friendship circle, there’s always one who’s a red lip devotee and one who steers clear). But no matter where you stand on the subject, there’s no denying it: the red lip is an enduring look that’s forever in fashion. And one of the most iconic products to achieve it? MAC Ruby Woo.

So popular is the shade that those in the know will immediately clock the brand as soon as you say the two words. Seasoned make-up artists know it by sight. But how exactly was Ruby Woo born and what makes it such a staple despite the thousands of red lipsticks now available on the market?

“As all the best things are, it was born from a happy accident,” MAC global senior artist Dominic Skinner tells Bazaar. He goes on to explain that in the late nineties, the brand’s product development team tried to tweak Russian Red (MAC’s bestselling red lipstick at the time) to make it less matte. Russian Red was quickly returned to its original formula due to backlash from loyal fans. In the process, however, the new base made the pigments brighter and more vivid. “This caught the eyes of the powers that be and the rest is MAC history.”

Ruby Woo was released in 1999 as part of the five-piece Retro Matte lipstick collection. It’s the only surviving member and it's been one of the brand’s bestsellers ever since, with seven tubes sold every minute globally. “Ruby Woo was the first MAC product I ever bought,” says Skinner, who discovered Ruby Woo while on a college assignment to find the reddest red lipstick. “I had never been to a MAC store before, but had heard it was the best place for pigment, payoff and quality. This is where I was introduced to Ruby Woo and I fell madly in love.”

While Ruby Woo is indeed the ‘reddest red’, what makes it universal is the hint of blue which makes it the perfect balance of warm and cool. “It’s vibrantly rich but it’s also a cool, crisp red that suits every skin tone. The ‘blue red’ helps to make it pop when worn and the retro matte texture means it’s totally absent of shine, leaving it velvet matte like a red petal.”

Ruby Woo enhances every undertone under the sun so it’s no wonder that celebrities such as Dita Von Teese, Angelina Jolie, Taylor Swift, Tracee Ellis Ross and Rihanna have all been said to wear the showstopping scarlet lipstick. The rich pigments also mean that there is no ashiness or patchiness upon application – and its a formula that has real staying power. Despite its groundbreaking beginnings, Ruby Woo is now a safe bet when it comes to red lipstick – which is a good thing considering beauty's fickle nature. Ask any beauty editor for red lip recommendations and you’re likely to have Ruby Woo on the list. It’s “classic Hollywood cool” says Dominic.

The Ruby Woo family has now grown to include various textures and formulas to please even the fussiest lipstick wearer. If you’ve always wanted to try Ruby Woo but found the texture too matte, Ruby New and Ruby Boo come in the Powder Kiss Lipstick and Powder Kiss Liquid Lipcolor textures respectively. Both feel featherlight on lips and give that romantic, blurred lip finish. Meanwhile, Ruby Phew is a Retro Matte Liquid Lipcolor with extra longevity. Ruby New is also available in the latest Powder Kiss Velvet Blur Slim Stick, a smooth formula that promises no flaking or feathering.

Whichever iteration you go for, rest assured that the shade will instantly elevate your look and spark that extra dose of joy. As Dominic says: “Trends come and go, products get reformulated, new technology gets developed. But Ruby Woo has never stopped working. I have used Ruby Woo everywhere and it never ceases to impress. From ball gown to jeans and a t-shirt, Ruby Woo just works.”
