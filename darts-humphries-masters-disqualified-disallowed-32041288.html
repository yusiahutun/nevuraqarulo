Luke Humphries has dart which lands in D17 disqualified by referee during Masters Darts

April 01, 2024
blog

Even the best in the business forget the rules from time to time.

World darts champion Luke Humphries is proof of that after coming a cropper at the 2024 Masters on Saturday. And it made for rare viewing as 'Cool Hand' even saw one of his throws disallowed en route to a 10-7 defeat against Stephen Bunting.

Humphries, 28, was trailing 9-7 and needed to clear 56 as Bunting stood on the verge of victory at the Marshall Arena. But after a wayward second dart spoiled his chances, he threw his third to hit double 17 while he was already stepping his way to the oche.

Referee Charlie Corstorphine spotted the overzealous movement and called the third dart ineligible just before Bunting put the finishing touches on his triumph. And spectators were largely in agreement that the official made the right call.

"Yep. Great refereeing that and rightly called out," nodded one in admiration of the decision. "Not a bad start for Charlie’s PDC career."

Humphries had to endure a chorus of boos and jeers throughout his efforts this weekend, which is a stark contrast from his title-winning displays at Alexandra Palace. And some of those critics were seemingly only too happy to see his brush with 'The Bullet' end in misery.
