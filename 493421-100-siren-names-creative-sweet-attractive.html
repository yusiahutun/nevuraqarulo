If you're into Greek mythology, you've probably heard of the siren legend. Sirens were humanlike beings with alluring voices luring sailors to doom with their sweet songs. In his poem, The Odyssey, the Greek poet Homer referred to them as mystic creatures, half bird and half human. So, if you're interested in the mythical world of magical creatures, what better way to express yourself than with siren names?

Siren names are derived from mythical creatures that roamed the seas in ancient times. Different cultures depicted these creatures differently. Some consider sirens and mermaids to be the same creature, while others distinguish between the two.

Classical Greeks thought of sirens as bird women with hidden knowledge, while others considered them evil temptresses who lure sailors to their deaths. Mermaids, on the other hand, were regarded as gentle, nonviolent beings who avoided human intervention.
