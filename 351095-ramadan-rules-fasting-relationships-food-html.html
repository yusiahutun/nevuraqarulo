Rules of Ramadan for unmarried couples

If you do not want an arranged marriage, remain friends with the one you like, and your end goal should be marriage. Here are more Ramadan rules on relationships of this kind:

  • Engaged couples should avoid each other and anything that could break their fast.
  • A wedding during Ramadan is allowed but not during Hajj.
  • Engaged couples should fast and break it with fellow Muslim brothers and sisters.
  • Ramzan wedding rules prohibit an engaged couple from sex.
  • The couple can kiss during Ramadan, but nothing more than that.
  • Do not sit near a girl or woman you have been ordered not to look at.
  • Do not perform prayers together.
  • Avoid fighting during Ramadan.
  • Refrain from triggering music and limit online communication with one another.
  • If the two can wait for marriage and not break the fast, they are allowed to celebrate Ramadan without being rushed into marriage.

Ramadan rules for married couples

Islam prohibits immorality and anything that tempts people to commit this sin but allows married couples to meet their natural needs.

  • Sex is allowed during Ramadan but not during the fast. You should fast for sixty more days or feed six poor people if you break the rule.
  • A married couple is allowed to kiss, but not to a particular extent.
  • Avoid marital conflicts.
  • You can only sit next to your wife, mother, and other blood relationships.

Why do we fast in Ramadan?

Fasting is among the five Islamic pillars that build and sustain a Muslim's faith in Allah. Hence, Ramadan fasting rules were not set to suppress people but to help them stay true to their fast for the entire month. Moreover, the penalties for violating some rules are not too harsh to handle.

Ramadan fasting rules

Allah established Ramzan fasting rules through Prophet Muhammad. He followed the guidelines and taught them to the devotees. Here are vital fasting rules Muslims abide by:

  • Eat and drink during Suhur (the pre-dawn meal) and Iftar (the evening meal after sunset).
  • Do not drink or eat until the prescribed time for breaking the fast.
  • The day's fast is broken at Iftar by eating dates. Prophet Muhammad ate them to break his fast.
  • If you unintentionally forget to fast or breaks it by eating or drinking, the fast is still valid as long as you continue to observe Sawm. You can choose to pay a Fidya.
  • If you intentionally break the fast without a worthy reason, the fast is invalidated, and you should pay Kaffarah.
  • Continue with your medication (pills and injections), for they will not invalidate your fast.
  • Vomiting due to sickness will not invalidate your fast because it is unintentional.
  • Vomiting on purpose is considered an intentional breaking of the fast.
  • Each young Muslim must observe the fast except for the sick or those travelling.
  • Breastfeeding women can skip the fast if they or the child feels weak.
  • The sick are exempted from the fast but should feed the poor for as many days as they did not fast.
  • No one is allowed to extend or shorten the fasting time.

Rules on food when fasting during Ramadan

There are several dos and don’ts set on Ramadan regarding food:

  • People who are not observing the fast should not eat in public.
  • Food should not be wasted during fasting or breaking time.
  • If you accidentally drink or eat, your fast is still valid because Allah forgives mistakes.
  • There are many Ramadan recipes to choose from when cooking traditional Ramadan foods. Hence, you are not restricted to specific recipes.

Ramadan rules about drinking water

Your fasting is still valid if you taste the water with your tongue without swallowing it. If you consume it, your fast will no longer be valid. Therefore;

  • You cannot drink water during the holy month as it will break your fast.
  • If you are not keeping the fast, do not drink water in front of the person who is observing fasting.
  • Do not take water while in public places during the holy month.

What happens if you don't pray during Ramadan?

No one is exempted from praying during Ramadan, but you can choose whether to fast with or without praying Salah. Only Allah subhanahu wa ta'ala will decide whether to accept or reject your fast.

Rules on prayer

  • You should pray five times.
  • Perform a Dua for breaking the fast during Ramadan.
  • Be in a state of concentration while praying because you are in Allah's presence.
  • Attempt a takmil (recitation of the Quran) as one of the religious observances of Ramadan. Recite at least one juz' per night in tarawih even though they are optional prayers (Sunnah).

Rules on cleanliness

Here are some basic rules about cleanliness during Ramadan:

  • Keep your clothes and house neat and shower regularly.
  • Avoid swallowing water while bathing, brushing your teeth, and washing because this will break the fast.
  • Complete ghusl (full-body wash) before sunrise to avoid invalidating the day’s fast.
  • Do not waste water when cleaning yourself, performing ablution, taking a bath, or other activities.

Rules on dressing

Muslims observe these dressing rules during Ramadan:

  • Wear clothes that are not revealing.
  • You can put on short-sleeved shirts but not shorts for they expose your body to people.
  • Women should leave their house while covered to prevent the men from seeing their bodies.
  • Women can only use eye drops and eyeliner.

Rules on periods

A majority of Muslim females understand Ramadan rules for periods. Here are some of them:

  • The woman can neither fast, perform the prayer, nor recite the Quran during periods.
  • If a woman opts not to keep the fast during periods, there is no penalty since she is forgiven.
  • If she chooses to keep the fast, she will be significantly awarded.
  • The woman should not touch the Quran. Since periods are not clean, their clothes might be dirty with blood.
  • Women or girls are not allowed to enter the mosque during their periods.

Other rules of Ramadan

There are more equally essential rules for Ramadan. New Muslim converts might not master all of them at once. It takes time to learn everything pertaining the Ramadan's guidelines.

  • Avoid insulting someone or using abusive language because this will damage the fast.
  • Islam generally forbids the consumption of alcohol. Therefore, do not drink whether it is the season of Ramadan or not.
  • Do not eat or drink in public if you are in an Islamic nation. In some states, you will be arrested.
  • Drawing blood for any reason or donation is also permissible during Ramadan.
  • Be generous with your money and food.
  • Conform to good thoughts.
  • Give to charity without anyone knowing.
