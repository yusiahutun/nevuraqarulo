Professional Career

Talking about her professional career, Ashleigh Ball made her television debut as Grubby in the 1987 television show “The Adventures of Teddy Ruxpin.” Additionally, she lent her voice to the character Mouse in the 1996 television show “Big Bag.”

Ball is known as a voice actress for voicing two of the main characters in the long-running fantasy series “My Little Pony: Friendship Is Magic” (2010-2019). She played the tomboy characters Applejack, a stubborn apple farmer, and Rainbow Dash, an overconfident speedster. Additionally, she appeared in the two characters in four “Equestria Girls” films (2013-2016) and several television specials. These versions were portrayed as adolescent musicians with emerging magical abilities.

Ball voices the role of adolescent inventor Mary Test in the science fantasy series “Johhny Test” and voiced the role from 2006 to 2014, while the character was previously voiced by Brittney Wilson. She voiced the role of Sissy Blakley for the same series and served as both a rival and a love interest to the protagonist.

In the short-lived series “Care Bears: Adventures in Care-a-lot” (2007-2008), she voiced the accident-prone Oopsy Bear. Additionally, the show was the third edition television series based on the “Care Bares” franchise. Oppsy first appeared in the computer-animated film “Care Bears: Oopsy Does It!” (2007), with Ball as his voice actress.

Besides acting, she is the lead singer of the indie, alternative rock, and synthpop band “Hey Ocean!” alongside guitarist David Beckingham and bassist David Vertesi. With the band, she provided her vocals to the release of their first album Stop Looking Like Music in 2006. It consisted of 11 tracks such as Beg Your Pardon, Thank You Very Much, Wise, and Eskimo Kisses.
