You have been with your girlfriend for two years. The relationship has had its challenges, but is mostly good. But there is one thing that has always clouded your relationship, "My girlfriend always finds faults in me." How can you handle the negativity?

All relationships have phases. At first, everything is a fairy tale characterised by intense passion and excitement in the presence of your partner. But when someone is always finding fault with you after being in a relationship for a while, it means you have reached the power struggle stage.

It is a phase where you start noticing differences and issues between you. The honeymoon is over, and you have woken up from your trance with a loud bang. Your relationship is at this stage, and you must figure out how to go to the third stage, also known as the stability stage.

Why does my girlfriend complain about everything I do? Here are some of the top reasons that can lead to this behaviour:

  • She is under a lot of stress and frustration
  • She is resentful towards you
  • She is overly dependent or needy
  • She has deeper emotional issues that she is not willing to address
  • She is looking for a connection, validation, or attention
  • She could also be wired to complain, which has become a habit

Why does my girlfriend criticize me all the time?

Criticism in a relationship can be damaging and lead to feelings of hurt, resentment, and demoralisation. When your girlfriend is criticising you all the time, these may be the reasons:

  • She addresses her concerns in a way that suggests she is finding fault with you.
  • She learned to communicate critically as a child or in other relationships.
  • She may have tried lodging complaints before, and it is not helping.
  • She may be afraid to trust her judgment in the relationship.
  • She may be traumatised from past experiences.
  • She feels insecure or inadequate.
  • She is projecting her issues onto you.
  • She may be trying to control you or the relationship.
  • She may be unhappy with herself or the relationship and is taking it out on you.
  • In other cases, she may not be aware of how her behaviour is affecting you and the relationship.

How to handle the negativity in a relationship

Whenever your girlfriend takes everything you say the wrong way, try to figure out how to handle the negativity. While it is not easy, here is what you can do to stabilise the situation:

1. Improve communication

Improving communication in a relationship can be challenging. Nevertheless, talk to your girlfriend about how her negativity affects you and your relationship. Admit you are not connecting as before, and talk with her about rekindling your connection.

2. Listen to her

When your girlfriend is negative, try listening to her perspective and understand where she is coming from. Listening to your girlfriend can help you build trust and intimacy. It also avoids misunderstandings and conflicts.

3. Be patient with her

If the negativity started recently, being patient with your girlfriend is best. It may take time for her to change her behaviour. But being patient shows her that you value her and your relationship enough to see beyond her faults.

4. Do not take it personally

When your girlfriend is negative about you, it can be easy to take it personally and feel hurt or defensive. However, remember that her negativity is likely not about you. Instead, it may be a reflection of her issues and struggles.

5. Focus on the positive

6. Set boundaries

Always set boundaries whenever your girlfriend constantly puts you down. Setting boundaries helps to protect your emotional well-being. It also helps you avoid being taken advantage of or mistreated by your girlfriend.

7. Do not engage in arguments

Arguments are unnecessary and unproductive when your relationship is in turmoil. When you engage in an argument, it only escalates the situation. Finding a solution will become impossible.

8. Be supportive

Despite the negativity, showing your girlfriend that you support and care about her is always healthy. Being supportive shows your girlfriend you care about her and value your relationship. That may go a long way in strengthening your bond.

9. Encourage her to seek help

Your girlfriendâ€™s negativity may be due to deeper issues unknown to you. And since you want to stabilise your relationship, it is best to encourage her to seek professional help. Professional help can give her the tools and resources to manage her negative thoughts and feelings.

10. Do not try to fix her

You cannot change a grownup, and your girlfriend is no exception. When you try to fix her, she will interpret the action as controlling or dismissive of her feelings. In the end, she will feel frustrated and resentful. It will also prevent her from taking responsibility for her actions.

11. Validate her feelings

Validating your girlfriend's feelings makes her feel understood and accepted. As a result, it can improve her mood and outlook. It will also make it easier for you to communicate your love and commitment to her, even in difficult times.

12. Do not dismiss her feelings

It is a bad idea to dismiss your girlfriendâ€™s feelings when she is negative about you. She will feel invalidated and unimportant, damaging her self-esteem and your relationship. It can also lead to misunderstandings and conflict in your relationship.

13. Do not bring up the past

Always avoid bringing up past issues when there is conflict in your relationship. Bringing up the past is unproductive and harmful and will only worsen things. The past will not help you find solutions to your current situation.

14. Work on yourself

Your girlfriend may be negative because of you. Instead of blaming everything on her, improve yourself and your behaviour. When you work on yourself, you will develop healthy coping mechanisms, confidence, and self-esteem.
