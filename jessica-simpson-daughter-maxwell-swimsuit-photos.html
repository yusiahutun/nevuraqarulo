See Jessica Simpson's Daughter Maxwell Rock a Swimsuit Like Her Mom!

August 21, 2024

Like mother, like daughter!

Jessica Simpson took to Instagram to share a series of sweet snapshots of daughter Maxwell -- and this pint-sized fashionista is the spitting image of her famous mama.

In one pic, the three-year-old tot is posing for a picture with her hand on her hip as she models a white and floral one-piece. Jess shared another snap of #MaxiDrew on the edge of the pool blowing a kiss to the camera.

It's clear that Maxi is just as comfortable in front of the camera as her gorgeous mother -- she's well on her way to a modeling career with those sassy moves!

In the latest Jessica Simpson Collection ad, which came out earlier this month, Maxwell's mom showed off her killer curves in plunging black swimsuit from her spring-summer collection.

The 34-year-old star hasn't been shy about showing off her super toned body. After welcoming Maxwell and son Ace back-to-back, the blonde bombshell got her body back into shape thanks to a rigorous workout routine.

"I work out five days a week: three days with a trainer, doing a mix of strength training and cardio, and then I do cardio on my own the other two days," she said in a recent interview with InStyle. "Somehow I make it work, but I've slowed down since my wedding."

Fitness is a priority for Jess, but she wants to teach her daughter how to have a positive body image—regardless of her size.

"Raising Maxwell makes me realize that I don't want her to see me beat myself up for things like food choices or numbers on a scale," she wrote in a 2013 blog post for Parents.com. "I don't want her to learn anything like that from me. Those things don't determine who we are and instead make us feel terrible about ourselves."

"I want to teach her to value herself, listen to herself and tune out the world," she continued. "I want her to know her value, rather than spending her energy fighting negative voices from within. I want to teach her to figure out what is truly right for her rather than worrying about what anyone else thinks."
