The much-awaited announcement was made by Raila, who is making a record fifth stab at the presidency, in a televised presser at the Kenyatta International Convention Centre (KICC).

Early life/Education

Karua was born on September 22, 1957, in the then Kirinyaga District in the Central Province.

She is the heir of Jackson Karua and his wife Josephine Wanjiru. She is the second born in a family of eight and was brought up deep in Kimunye village.

Raila's running mate started off her education at Mugumo Primary School. In 1974, she commenced her O Levels at Karoti Girls Secondary School.

The former Gichugu MP then proceeded to Nairobi Girls School for other A Levels. In 1977, Karua joined the University of Nairobi where she pursued Law.

Between 1980 and 1981, she was enrolled at the Kenya School of Law for the statutory postgraduate law course that is a prerequisite to admission to the Kenyan roll of advocates and licensing to practise law in Kenya.

In 2011, she pursued Global Executive MBA at the United States International University (USIU)-Africa, in Nairobi.

Legal Career

Between 1981 to 1987 after graduating, Karua worked as a magistrate in various courts including those at Makadara, Nakuru and Kibera. During her service, she received credit for her careful discernment.

In 1987, she resolved to start her own law firm, Martha Karua &Co. Advocates and among the cases she handled include the treason trial of Koigi Wamwere and that of MP Mirugi Kariuki.

Karua defended several human rights activists and her firm risked being blacklisted by the then President Daniel Moi government.

Entry into politics

As a vocal lawyer, Karua was a member of the opposition political movements that successfully agitated for the re-introduction of the multi-party democracy in Kenya in the 1990s.

At that time, the country was under the authoritarian rule of the Kenya African National Union (KANU) as the only legally recognised political party. The outfit was led by Moi.

She then joined Kenneth Matiba's Ford Asili party before decamping to the Democratic Party of Kenya (DP) where she won the party ticket in 1992 and proceeded to win the Gichugu parliamentary seat in the hotly contested polls.

Being the first woman lawyer elected to Parliament, she was appointed as DP's legal affairs secretary.

Between 2001-2002, she was nominated by Parliament as a member of the select committee on Constitutional Review.

Later on, she, together with others formed the NARC political coalition that won the 2002 General Election under the leadership of Mwai Kibaki, ending KANU's nearly four decades in Kenyan leadership.

Between January 2003 and December 2005, Kibaki elected her as the Minister of Water.

In 2008, after the controversial 2007 General Election that culminated in chaos, Kibaki appointed her as the Minister for Justice and Constitutional Affairs.

It is worth noting that Karua accused Raila, her now political friend, of planning the chaos that led to the killing of over one thousand people.

Running for president

In November 2008, Karua was endorsed as the national chairperson of the NARC Kenya political; party.

After her endorsement, she immediately declared she would be gunning for the presidency in the 2013 General Election.

In 2009, she resigned as Minister of Justice and Constitutional Affairs on 6 April 2009, citing frustrations in discharging her duties, for instance when Kibaki appointed Judges without her knowledge.

In her 2013 presidential bid, in which she vied under the Narc Kenya party ticket, she came a distant sixth with 43,881 votes in the highly contested seat that President Uhuru Kenyatta emerged a victor.

Karua then made a comeback into politics in the 2017 General Election when she vied for the Kirinyaga gubernatorial seat.

She was however floored by Anne Waiguru but disputed the outcome citing election irregularities.

After her appeal was thrown out, she stayed out of politics but continued with her legal career. She was one of the lawyers that participated in the Building Bridges Initiative legal battle.

In early 2022, during the political re-alignments in readiness for the August polls, Karua joined the Raila led Azimio.

Coming from the vote-rich Mt Kenya region, she immediately became a frontrunner in the running mate race.

Controversies

In December 2015, Karua admitted to receiving a KSh 2 million "donation" to her presidential campaign costs from British American Tobacco. The cash was allegedly paid to stop action against cigarette smuggling.

At one time in her Kirinyaga District when KANU was still in power, Karua walked out on Moi who was then addressing a crowd in the district stadium.

In February 2009 during her time as Minister of Justice, she once had a heated argument with the Minister of Agriculture William Ruto at a cabinet meeting as Kibaki sat quietly. Karua demanded Ruto's resignation over a maize scandal.
