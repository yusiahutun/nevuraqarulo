Patti Murin is a famous actress, singer, and dancer from the United States. Patti Murin is widely known for her appearance in the 91st Macy’s Thanksgiving Day Parade in 2017 and in the TV series ‘Chicago Med’.

Early Life

Patti Murin was born in Hopewell Junction, New York, United States on the 28th of September 1980. She is 43 years old with the zodiac sign Libra. Murin holds an American nationality and follows the Christian religion. Besides that, Patti has not disclosed any information related to her family identity or history.

Patti Murin – Education

Speaking of her educational background, Murin completed her secondary education at a local high school in America. Following that, she enrolled at Syracuse University where she graduated with a degree in musical theatre.

Career and Professional Life

Talking about her career, In Ft. Lauderdale, Florida, Murin made her stage debut as Polly in the regional theater production of Crazy for You.

She made her Off-Broadway debut with York Theatre Company in the 2005 staged concert of the Elizabeth Diggs, Tom Jones, and Harvey Schmidt stage musical Mirette. Her next role was Belle in a Beauty and the Beast national tour (2006).

In the Broadway production of Xanadu, Murin debuted in 2007–2008 first as an understudy, swing, and finally as a substitute for Clio/Kira.

In 2012, she made her Broadway debut in the lead role of Lysistrata Jones. She performed as Glinda in the first national tour of Wicked (2012)–2013, in addition to various roles in regional and other stage productions.

Moreover, On the NBC medical drama series Chicago Med, which she joined in 2016 and left in 2017, Murin portrayed pathologist Nina Shore.

She starred as the first Princess Anna in the Broadway production of the musical Frozen, which debuted in March 2018 at the St. James Theatre.

She also made an appearance as Anna at the Buell Theatre in Denver, Colorado, for the 2017 out-of-town tryouts and workshops. On February 16, 2020, Murin gave the part her last performance.

Furthermore, She co-stars with her husband Colin Donnell in the January 2020 Hallmark Channel movie Love on Iceland.
