Pecs appeal: Cheryl, Gwen and Gwyneth lead the anti-cleavage brigade in plunging necklines

We are no strangers to seeing acres of exposed flesh on the red carpet. Whatever the occasion, a judicious scooped neckline that reveals a healthy cleavage is guaranteed to get the flashbulbs popping.

But according to Cheryl Cole, Gwen Stefani and Gwyneth Paltrow, the hot new trend is for flat, not inflated chests.

The bony-chested triumvirate took to the carpet this week in deep plunging dresses with dramatically flattened décolletages, proving the new must-have cleavage is no cleavage. 

Bony: Gwyneth Paltrow, Cheryl Cole and Gwen Stefani seem to be celebrating the 'anti-cleavage' movement

The essential accompaniment to the anti-cleavage trend? An ultra-deep plunging dress. Much more revealing than a usual plunge, the neckline is cut in a deep, wide V-shape to expose a wide area of décolletage which is arranged so that no hint of a cleavage appears.

The flat-chested look - often achieved thanks to lashings of Tit Tape to keep assets under strict control -  means that even the deepest plunge can be worn without seeming tacky, and is possible even in women who can usually fill a C-cup. 

Exposure: Both Gwyneth Paltrow and Cheryl Cole have been keener on showing off their cleavages in the past

Appearing on the red carpet at Cannes, Cheryl appears much flatter-chested than recent photos would suggest, though her plunging white Stephane Rolland dress does allow a faint hint of a curve.

There's no such half-measures for Gwen in her Stella McCartney jumpsuit and Gwen in her Roksanda Illincic dress, who demonstrate the look at its most extreme, their breast-free aerobicised chest area highlighted by their dramatic plunging necklines.

The A-listers' big boob backlash comes in the fake-tanned face of pumped-up glamour girls of The Only Way Is Essex, Jersey Shores and other ubiquitous over-bronzed starlets.

Their cup runneth over: Could celebrities like TOWIE's Amy Childs and glamour girl Katie Price have devalued the cleavage by exposing so much flesh in their revealing outfits?

The more lesser celebrities make their mark by displaying their assets, the more big breasts have become devalued to the point of being unattractive.

The solution? The corralled-in, strapped-down 'anti-cleavage'.

Just ask Victoria Beckham. Since making her move into the world of fashion design, she has reduced her breasts and refrained from wearing low-cut styles. As a result, she has been taken far more seriously by the fashion glitterati.

Transformation: Victoria Beckham's cup-size reduction and less-revealing wardrobe coincided with her being taken more seriously by the fashion world

Even Katie Price has toned down her look of late, frequently choosing to cover up on with thin sweaters and high-necked blouses rather than exposing the usual acres of flesh with revealing outfits. 

Cachet in the celebrity world comes now, it seems from the sparrow chest of the anti-cleavage brigade, not the puffed-up peahen look of the B-list.

So after years of silicone worship, will this flat-chest trend catch on? We'll have to wait for the next series of The Only Way is Essex to find out.
